Task 05

Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

In the blog, the writer says: "I have traveled to 15 countries" but also "I arrived here two days ago." Why?

Rule 1: The Life Experience (Unfinished Time)

We use the Present Perfect Simple (have + V3) to talk about general experiences in our life up to NOW. We don't say when it happened. The time is unfinished (your life).

Rule 2: The Specific Event (Finished Time)

We use the Past Simple (V2) to talk about completed actions at a specific, finished time in the past (yesterday, in 2020, two days ago).

2023 (Finished Time)
"I went to Paris in 2023."
(Past Simple)
My Whole Life ➔
"I have been to Paris twice."
(Present Perfect)

Task 07

The Future Continuous

In the blog, the writer says: "Tomorrow at this exact time, I will be riding the bullet train."

We use the Future Continuous (will be + V-ing) to talk about an action that will be in progress at a specific time in the future. You are imagining yourself in the middle of doing it.

NOW (10:00 AM)
Tomorrow (10:00 AM)
Flight takes off at 9 AM, lands at 1 PM.
At 10 AM, I will be flying.

Task 11

Pronunciation: The Invisible 'Have'

In spoken English, the auxiliary verb 'have' in the Present Perfect is almost always contracted and reduced.

  • I have been... ➔ "I've been" ➔ Sounds like: /aɪv bɪn/
  • You have seen... ➔ "You've seen" ➔ Sounds like: /juv sin/
  • He has gone... ➔ "He's gone" ➔ Sounds like: /hiz gɔn/
  • We have tried... ➔ "We've tried" ➔ Sounds like: /wiv traɪd/

Practice saying the sentences from Task 6 aloud, using these fast reductions.
