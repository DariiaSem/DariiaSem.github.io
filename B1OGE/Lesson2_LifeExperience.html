Task 7: Grammar Theory 1

Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

Review the differences between these two past tenses based on the rules.

Tense Usage Example
Present Perfect

(have/has + past participle)		 Experience in general: Used to say that something *has happened* in your life, without saying *when*. I have been to Paris.
She has learned calligraphy.
Not finished / Result now: Actions that started in the past and continue to now, or have a direct result now. I have played tennis for 10 years. (I still play)
He is happy. He has passed his test.
Past Simple

(verb + ed / irregular)		 Specific / Finished Event: You give details about a specific event that is completely finished. I played tennis for 3 years. (I don't play anymore)
I bought this bag there.
Time is given: When you state exactly *when* something happened. I was at school yesterday.
She went to Japan in 2021.

Task 11: Grammar Theory 2

Used to vs. Get used to

Review the rules for talking about past habits versus adapting to new situations.

Structure Usage Examples
Used to + verb Past habits and states, particularly for the distant past that are no longer true now. (+) I used to train three times a week.
(-) I didn't use to be good at football.
(?) Did you use to play here?
Be / Get used to + -ing (or noun) To talk about a situation that is familiar or the process of becoming familiar with something new. I didn't like being the goalkeeper at first but now I'm used to it.
I am gradually getting used to waking up early.
