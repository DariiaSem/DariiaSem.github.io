Task 4: Reading

The Jump

Read the story about a woman's first base jump. Pay close attention to the sentences with "If".

Standing on the edge of the 1000-foot bridge, Maria felt sick. She remembered her instructor's rule: "If the wind blows faster than 15mph, we don't jump." But today, the air was perfectly still.

Her instructor looked at her. "If you jump today, you will experience the greatest thrill of your life," he smiled. Maria hesitated. "If I were you, I would just count to three and step forward," he added calmly.

She took a deep breath and leaped. The feeling was exhilarating. Later that evening, sitting safely in a cafe, she laughed. "If I hadn't taken that step, I would have regretted it forever."

Task 6: Grammar Theory

Real Conditionals (0 & 1st)

Conditionals have two parts: the If-clause (the condition) and the Main clause (the result). We use the 0 and 1st conditionals for situations that are REAL or highly possible.

The REAL Branch

Zero Conditional

Facts, rules, general truths.

If + Present Simple,
Present Simple

"If the wind blows fast, we don't jump."

First Conditional

Real future possibilities.

If + Present Simple,
Will + Base Verb

"If you jump today, you will love it."

Rule: We NEVER use "will" in the "If" clause.
❌ If it will rain, I will stay inside.
✅ If it rains, I will stay inside.

Task 8: Grammar Theory

Unreal Conditionals (2nd & 3rd)

We use the 2nd and 3rd conditionals for situations that are UNREAL (imaginary). Look at the visual timeline below to see how the grammar shifts back in time.

The UNREAL Branch

Second Conditional

Imaginary Present/Future. (Giving advice/dreaming)

If + Past Simple,
Would + Base Verb

"If I were you, I would jump."

Third Conditional

Imaginary Past. (Regrets or alternate history)

If + Past Perfect,
Would have + V3

"If I hadn't jumped, I would have cried."

Task 10: Pronunciation

Intonation in Conditionals

According to Teaching American English Pronunciation, complex sentences require specific pitch changes. Let's learn and practice!

The Rising & Falling Pattern

1. The If-Clause: The pitch goes UP (⤴) at the end of the clause, followed by a short pause. This signals to the listener: "Wait, I'm not finished speaking yet."
2. The Main Clause: The pitch goes DOWN (⤵) at the end, signaling that the thought is complete.

Listen & Repeat:

1. If I were an adrenaline junkie ⤴, I would try base jumping. ⤵

2. If she had been more meticulous ⤴, the catastrophe wouldn't have happened. ⤵

Task 14: Discussion

The Ethics of Extreme Sports

Discuss these deeper ethical questions with your partner. Try to use advanced conditionals (2nd and 3rd) where possible.

Rescue Costs

If an extreme athlete gets stuck on a mountain and needs a helicopter rescue, should the government pay for it, or should the athlete pay the massive bill themselves?

Parental Rules

If you were a parent and your teenager wanted to take up a perilous sport (like base jumping), would you allow them to do it? Why or why not?

Banning Sports?

Some people argue that highly fatal sports should be illegal. If the government banned these sports, what would the repercussions be?
