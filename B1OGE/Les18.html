04. Structural Blueprint

Passive Voice: Present & Past

In urban planning, the action is more important than the politician who did it. We use the passive voice when the "doer" is obvious, unknown, or unimportant.

The Core Matrix

Subject + TO BE (am/is/are/was/were) + Past Participle (V3)
Tense Formula Setup Clinical Example
Present Simple
(Facts, routines, current states)		 is / are + V3 "The city streets are cleaned every night."
"This neighborhood is heavily polluted by traffic."
Past Simple
(Finished historical events)		 was / were + V3 "The old transit tunnel was demolished in 2018."
"These houses were built by the river."

⚠️ Syntax Safety Note: Never omit the 'to be' verb. Do not say: "The subway opened last year." (This implies the subway is a living creature that opened itself). It must read: "The subway was opened."

05. Precision Drill

The Architect's Ledger

Transform these active municipal logs into the passive voice to make them sound professional and objective. Pay attention to singular vs. plural!

  1. Active: The government collects recycling every Tuesday.
    Passive: The recycling every Tuesday.
  2. Active: The architects designed the new sustainable park last year.
    Passive: The new sustainable park last year.
  3. Active: Heavy traffic congestion causes severe air pollution.
    Passive: Severe air pollution by heavy traffic congestion.
  4. Active: They didn't finish the infrastructure projects on time.
    Passive: The infrastructure projects on time.
