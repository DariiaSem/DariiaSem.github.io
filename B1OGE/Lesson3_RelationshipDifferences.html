Task 12: Grammar Theory

Making Comparisons

When we compare how different people behave, we use comparatives and superlatives. Let's review the rules.

Type Rule Example
Short Adjectives Add -er for comparatives.
Add the -est for superlatives.		 Men are faster than women at this task.
It is the hardest test.
Long Adjectives (2+ syllables) Use more / less for comparatives.
Use the most / the least for superlatives.		 Women are more careful.
This is the most important thing.
Equality Use as + adjective + as to say things are the same. Use not as...as for differences. Women are as smart as men.
He is not as tall as his brother.
Exceptions:
Good → Better → The Best
Bad → Worse → The Worst
Far → Further → The Furthest

The Great Debate

Imagine you are driving to a holiday destination. You are lost! Follow the flowchart to argue about stereotypes and navigating.

↓
Person B: Defend yourself! Say men/women are better at navigating naturally.
↓

↓
