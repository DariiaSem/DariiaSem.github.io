Work Habits & Planning

Interactive Format: Tap or hover over the cards below to reveal the definitions of these advanced workplace words.

Triage

(verb / noun)

To sort tasks or problems by urgency, deciding which are the most important to deal with first.

Micro-manage

(verb)

To control every single, small detail of an employee's work in an annoying or restrictive way.

Expedite

(verb)

To make an action or process happen sooner or be accomplished more quickly.

Squander

(verb)

To waste time, money, or opportunities in a reckless or foolish manner.

Bottleneck

(noun)

A point of congestion in a system that slows down the overall process.

Haphazard

(adjective)

Lacking any obvious principle of organization; random and messy.

Task 4: Reading

The Personality Test

Read the two profiles below. Which one describes your working style?

Profile A: The Planner 📝

A planner will spend the night before work making lists, prioritizing, and making sure everything is under control. They arrive early and get started on their first task immediately. They hate distractions and prefer to do one thing at a time. Planners are highly organized but can panic if their schedule suddenly changes.

Profile B: The Procrastinator ⏳

A procrastinator prefers to see what happens. They put off doing difficult jobs until later. They are more likely to arrive at work just in time, with a coffee in their hand and a stressed look on their face. They tend to get distracted easily and squander time, but they can work incredibly fast when a deadline is very close.

Task 9: Pronunciation

Reductions: "Gonna" & The Schwa

According to Teaching American English Pronunciation (Avery & Ehrlich), native speakers rarely pronounce every sound in fast speech. Let's look at a very common reduction used for future plans.

Going to ➔ Gonna

When "going to" is used as an auxiliary verb for the future, the "ing" and the "to" merge into a weak Schwa /ə/ sound.
Written: I am going to buy it.
Spoken: I'm gən-nə buy it. (gonna)

Note: We do NOT reduce it when "going" is the main verb of motion (e.g., "I am going to the shop").

Task 11: Reading

Predicting the Future 🚗

We use "will" to make predictions about the future. Read this text about future technology.

The Commute of the Future

In the year 2050, the concept of driving a car will be completely different. The car of the future will use purely electric or solar energy, so it will be silent and clean. In fact, you won't need to drive it at all; it will drive itself.

You will just tell the computer your destination, and you can sit back and read a book or catch up on sleep. A lot of this technology already exists. However, because of current infrastructure, you probably won't see these fully autonomous "living rooms on wheels" everywhere for another 20 years. Driving will be much less stressful in the future!
