Syntax Base · Task 07

Advanced Comparatives & Modifiers

In B1+, we don't just say "A is bigger than B." We specify how much bigger. Is it a massive difference, or a tiny one?

Small Difference
a bit / slightly / a little
Huge Difference
much / far / way / a lot
Modifier Structure Tech Example
Big Difference much / far / way / a lot + Comparative (+ than) "Fiber optic internet is far faster than dial-up."
Small Difference slightly / a bit / a little + Comparative (+ than) "The iPhone 15 is slightly lighter than the 14."
Exact Similarity as + adjective + as "My new laptop is as heavy as a brick."
Small Inequality not quite as + adjective + as "Tablets are not quite as powerful as desktops." (Almost, but not 100%)
Huge Inequality nowhere near as + adjective + as "A smartwatch is nowhere near as expensive as a car." (Not even close)
Execution · Task 08

Upgrade the Sentences

Make these basic comparisons sound advanced by adding the modifier in brackets.

  • Basic: Virtual Reality is more immersive than regular gaming.
    Advanced: [far] Virtual Reality is regular gaming.
  • Basic: This new update is better than the old one.
    Advanced: [slightly] This new update is the old one.
  • Basic: Writing an email is not as fast as sending a text.
    Advanced: [nowhere near] Writing an email is sending a text.
  • Basic: The battery life on this model is worse.
    Advanced: [way] The battery life on this model is .

Simulation · Task 12

The Silicon Valley Pitch

Student A is a Startup Founder pitching a ridiculous, highly-priced new gadget. Student B is a skeptical Billionaire Investor.

Role A: The Founder

You have invented the "Smart Pillow." It costs $800. It tracks brain waves and plays ads in your dreams. You must convince the investor it is far more advanced and way better than a normal pillow.

Role B: The Investor

You hate the idea. It sounds useless and invasive. Use comparatives to attack it ("A normal pillow is nowhere near as expensive") and modals of obligation ("You must prove people want this").
