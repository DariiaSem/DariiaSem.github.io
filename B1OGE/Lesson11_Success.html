Task 5: Grammar Theory

Present Perfect Simple

Review the structure and uses of the Present Perfect Simple tense.

Form Structure Examples
Statement (+) have/has + past participle I/you/we/they have flown...
He/she/it has flown...
Negative (-) have/has + not + past participle I haven't flown...
She hasn't flown...
Question (?) Have/Has + subject + past participle Have you flown...?
Has she flown...?

When do we use it?

Use Example
Situations and states that started in the past and are still true She's had her motorbike for over six years.
A series of actions continuing up to now We've travelled by taxi, bus, plane and train - all in the last twenty-four hours!
Completed actions at a time in the past which is not mentioned Have you ever flown in a helicopter?
Completed actions where the important thing is the present result I've booked the coach tickets.
US vs UK Grammar:
Speakers of American English often use the past simple in situations where speakers of British English would use the present perfect simple.
US: We already saw the Sphinx.
UK: We've already seen the Sphinx.
Task 6: Grammar Theory

Present Perfect Continuous

Now, let's look at the continuous form.

Form Structure Examples
Statement (+) have/has + been + V-ing I have been travelling...
He has been travelling...
Negative (-) have/has + not + been + V-ing I haven't been travelling...
Question (?) Have/Has + subject + been + V-ing Have you been travelling...?

When do we use it?

Use Example
Actions continuing up to the present moment We have been driving for hours. Can't we have a break soon?
Actions stopping just before the present moment (explaining a present result) I'm out of breath because I've been running to get here in time.
Watch Out!
The present perfect continuous is not normally used with the words ever and never.
✅ Have you ever flown in a helicopter before?
❌ Have you ever been flying in a helicopter before?
