Task 5: Grammar Theory

Modals of Deduction

When we don't know the exact truth, we use "Modals of Deduction" to guess. We choose the word based on how sure we are.

How to Guess in English

99% Sure (YES) MUST BE

"He has a Ferrari. He must be rich."

50% Sure (MAYBE) MIGHT / COULD BE

"She is late. She might be stuck in traffic."

99% Sure (NO) CAN'T BE

"He is 10 years old. He can't be a doctor."

🚨 IMPORTANT: We NEVER use "mustn't" to guess that something is false. We always use "can't".

Task 6: Pronunciation

Fast Speech

In fast, spoken English, the "T" at the end of these modals is often silent when followed by a consonant like "B".

Must be

Sounds like: "Mus-bee"

Can't be

Sounds like: "Cam-bee"

Listen & Repeat:

"It must be true... No, it can't be!"

Task 8: Reading

The Ghost Ship

Read the true story of the Mary Celeste. It is one of the most famous unsolved mysteries in history.

The Mystery of the Mary Celeste

In November 1872, the ship Mary Celeste sailed from New York to Italy. The captain, his wife, his young daughter, and seven sailors were on board.

One month later, another ship found the Mary Celeste floating alone in the Atlantic Ocean. The sailors went onto the ship to investigate. They found no one. The captain and his family had vanished. However, the ship was in perfect condition. There was plenty of food and water for six months. The captain's money and jewelry were still on the desk. But one small lifeboat was missing.

Nobody ever saw the people from the Mary Celeste again. To this day, nobody knows why they left a perfectly safe ship to get into a tiny wooden boat in the middle of the ocean.
