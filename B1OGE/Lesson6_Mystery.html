Task 1: The Memory Check

The Detective's Memory

In Lesson 5, we learned how to guess about the present using Modals of Deduction (Must be, Might be, Can't be). Read the clues and make a deduction.

  • Clue: Your neighbor, who works part-time at a bakery, just drove home in a brand new, $200,000 Ferrari.
    Deduction: He (must be / can't be) very rich! Maybe he won the lottery.
  • Clue: The new Italian restaurant in town is completely empty on a Saturday night.
    Deduction: The food (can't be / might be) very good.
  • Clue: You hear a strange noise in the kitchen, but you live alone. You have a cat.
    Deduction: It (might be / must be) a burglar, or it could just be the cat playing.

Task 5: Reading

The Ghost Ship

Read the true story of the Mary Celeste. It is one of the most famous maritime mysteries in history.

INCIDENT LOG: 1872

In November 1872, the ship Mary Celeste sailed from New York to Italy. The captain, his wife, his young daughter, and seven sailors were on board. The weather was recorded as relatively calm.

One month later, another ship found the Mary Celeste floating alone in the Atlantic Ocean. The sailors went onto the ship to investigate. They found no one. The captain and his family had vanished. However, the ship was in perfect condition. There was plenty of food and fresh water for six months. The captain's money and personal items were still on the desk.

The only thing missing was one small wooden lifeboat. Nobody ever saw the ten people from the Mary Celeste again. To this day, nobody knows why they decided to abandon a perfectly safe ship to get into a tiny boat in the middle of the ocean.

Task 7: Grammar Theory

Past Modals of Deduction

How do we guess about things that happened in the PAST? We use the exact same modal verbs, but we add have + V3 (Past Participle).

How Sure Are You? Structure Example (The Mary Celeste)
99% Sure (YES) Must have + V3 They took the lifeboat, so they must have left calmly.
50% Sure (MAYBE) Might / Could have + V3 They wrote a weird note. The captain might have gone crazy.
99% Sure (NO) Can't have + V3 The money was there. They can't have been attacked by pirates.
🚨 IMPORTANT RULE: We NEVER use "mustn't have" to guess that something didn't happen. We always use can't have or couldn't have!

Task 9: Reading

The Great Hijacking ✈️

Read about D.B. Cooper, the only unsolved airplane hijacking in American history.

INCIDENT LOG: 1971

In 1971, a man bought a plane ticket using the fake name "Dan Cooper". During the flight, he showed the flight attendant a bomb in his briefcase. He demanded $200,000 in cash and four parachutes.

When the plane landed in Seattle, he took the money, released the passengers, and told the pilot to fly toward Mexico. Somewhere over the dark forests of Washington state, D.B. Cooper opened the back door of the airplane and jumped out into the freezing, rainy night with the money.

The FBI investigated for 45 years. They found a small amount of the money buried in the sand near a river years later, but they never found Cooper. Did he survive the jump? Did he live a rich life in secret, or did he die in the forest?

Task 13: Pronunciation

"Must've, Might've, Can't've"

In fast, spoken English, we heavily reduce the word "have" when it comes after a modal verb. We drop the "H" sound completely.

Must have

sounds like:
"MUST-uv"

Might have

sounds like:
"MITE-uv"

Can't have

sounds like:
"CANT-uv"

Listen & Repeat:

"They must've left... they might've gone to the island... they can't've survived!"

There is a hot cup of tea on the table, and steam is coming out of it. Her jacket and purse are still on the sofa. But your friend is nowhere inside the house.

What happened?

  • She (go) far away, because her tea is still hot. (99% NO)
