Task 04. Editorial Reading

The Antwerp Diamond Heist

Read the true-crime report below. Deduce the meaning of the highlighted words. Notice how the author uses passives to describe actions occurring over time.

Case File #448-B • Department of Justice

It was known as the heist of the century. In 2003, over $100 million in loose diamonds, gold, and jewelry disappeared from the heavily fortified Antwerp Diamond Center. The perpetrators bypassed ten levels of security, including infrared heat detectors, a seismic sensor, and a lock with 100 million possible combinations.

At the exact moment the vault was being emptied, the local police were being distracted by a fake emergency call across town. The thieves did not embezzle digital funds; they carried the physical diamonds out in garbage bags.

For months, the criminal mastermind, Leonardo Notarbartolo, claimed he had a flawless alibi. He hoped to be exonerated by the courts. However, DNA evidence had been left on a half-eaten sandwich near the crime scene.

Eventually, Notarbartolo was indicted and sent to prison, but he refused to name his accomplices. To this day, the majority of the stolen diamonds have never been recovered.

Task 07. Structural Blueprint

Passive Voice: Continuous & Perfect

To sound fluent in reporting (like a news anchor or detective), you must master how to use passives for ongoing actions (Continuous) and actions completed before now (Perfect).

1. Continuous Passives (Ongoing Actions)

am/is/are/was/were + BEING + Past Participle (V3)
Present Continuous
(Happening right now)		 "The suspect is being interrogated by the detective right now."
Past Continuous
(Happening at a specific past time)		 "At midnight, the security cameras were being disabled."

2. Perfect Passives (Completed before a point)

has/have/had + BEEN + Past Participle (V3)
Present Perfect
(Completed before NOW)		 "The stolen diamonds have never been recovered."
Past Perfect
(Completed before the PAST)		 "When police arrived, the vault had already been emptied."
