03. Editorial Reading

Escaping the Metropolis

Read the text below. Deduce the meaning of the highlighted words from their context. Pay attention to how the author describes actions where the "doer" is not mentioned.

Urban Planning • By Sarah Jenkins

After ten years in a bustling metropolis, Sarah decided she could no longer handle the daily two-hour commute. The constant traffic congestion and noise were severely detrimental to her mental health. She packed her bags and moved to a quaint village in the mountains.

However, she soon realized that escaping the city wasn't so simple. The rural infrastructure was severely lacking—the internet was incredibly slow, and there were no sustainable transport options like electric buses or trains.

Meanwhile, her old neighborhood in the city underwent rapid gentrification. The local bakeries were replaced by luxury boutiques. Old apartment buildings were demolished, and rent prices are constantly raised. The original residents are slowly pushed out of their homes.

Sarah sometimes misses the energy of the city, but she knows that modern urban areas are designed for commerce, not for peace.

05. Syntactical Architecture

The Passive Voice: Present & Past

In city planning, the action is often more important than the person doing it. We use the passive voice when we don't know who did the action, or when it doesn't matter.

Rule 1: Focus on the Action, Not the Actor

Active: The government builds new roads every year.
Passive: New roads are built every year. (We don't care about the government; we care about the roads).

Form: The Verb "To Be" + Past Participle (V3)

Present Simple Passive am / is / are + V3 The streets are cleaned every night.
The city is polluted by cars.
Past Simple Passive was / were + V3 The bridge was designed in 1990.
The old houses were demolished.
💡 Check: You must always have the verb to be. If you just say "The bridge built in 1990", it means the bridge was a magical living creature that built something else! It must be "The bridge was built."
06. Precision Drill

The City Archives

Change the following active sentences into the Passive Voice (Present or Past). Pay close attention to singular/plural and the tense.

  1. Active: They collect the recycling on Tuesdays.
    Passive: The recycling on Tuesdays.
  2. Active: The architect designed the new sustainable park last year.
    Passive: The new sustainable park last year.
  3. Active: Heavy traffic congestion causes severe air pollution.
    Passive: Severe air pollution by heavy traffic congestion.
  4. Active: They didn't finish the infrastructure project on time.
    Passive: The infrastructure project on time.
