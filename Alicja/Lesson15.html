Task 2: Reading Analysis

The Trap of Lifestyle Inflation 💸

Read the text about managing increased income as a senior doctor.

As young doctors transition from public hospital salaries to private practice, they often encounter "Lifestyle Inflation." This occurs when an increase in income leads to a parallel increase in spending. Suddenly, upgrading the car, eating at expensive restaurants, and taking luxury holidays becomes the norm. While enjoying the fruits of your labor is important, failing to build a financial nest eggzaskórniaki/oszczędności can leave you trapped. Financial advisors often suggest that doctors outsourcezlecać na zewnątrz their investments and focus on building equitykapitał/wartość majątku netto, perhaps by saving for a down paymentzaliczka/wkład własny on a clinic space rather than a sports car. This approach ensures long-term wealth and helps prevent financial burnoutwypalenie zawodowe.

Discussion: Do you agree with this assessment? Have you noticed "lifestyle inflation" among your medical peers in Poland?

Task 4: Grammar Theory

Causative Verbs 🛠️

We use these when we arrange for someone else to do something for us. Essential for daily life admin!

Structure Usage Example (Daily Life)
Have + object + V3 You pay or ask a professional to do a service for you. "I had my apartment painted last week."
(I didn't paint it myself)
Get + object + V3 Same as above, but slightly more informal and common in spoken English. "I need to get my taxes done before Friday."
Make + person + base verb To force or require someone to do something. "The bank made me sign fifty documents."

Task 7: Grammar Theory

Mixed Conditionals 🔀

We use mixed conditionals when the time in the 'if' clause is different from the time in the result clause.

Type Structure Example (Reflecting on Life)
Past Condition → Present Result
(You did something in the past, and it affects you NOW)		 If + Past Perfect,
would + infinitive		 "If I hadn't gone to London, I wouldn't speak English so well today."
Present Condition → Past Result
(A general truth about you affected a past event)		 If + Past Simple,
would have + V3		 "If I wasn't a doctor, I would have studied law years ago."
Task 8: Grammar Practice

"What If...?" 🤔

Complete the mixed conditional sentences.

  • 1. If I (save) more money during my internship, I (have) a bigger down payment now. had saved / would have
  • 2. If she (be) a more confident person, she (demand) a higher salary yesterday. was (were) / would have demanded
  • 3. If we (not / buy) that expensive car, we (not / struggle) to pay the mortgage today. hadn't bought / wouldn't be struggling
