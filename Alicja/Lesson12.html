Task 3

Reading: The Bleeding Patient 🩸

Antepartum haemorrhage requires rapid differentiation. Placenta Praevia typically presents as painless, bright red bleeding. It is often diagnosed incidentally on the 20-week anomaly scan.

Conversely, a Placental Abruption can be insidiouspodstępny. The bleeding may be concealedukryty (no vaginal blood loss). The classic presentation is dark red bleeding with a woodytwardy jak deska, tense, and highly painful uterus. This is a profound obstetric emergency often leading to fetal distress.

Q4. The "Woody" Uterus

Why does the uterus feel "woody" during an abruption?
Answer: Blood infiltrates the myometrium (Couvelaire uterus), causing severe tetanic contractions.

Q5. The Digital Exam Rule

Why is a digital vaginal examination strictly contraindicated in suspected Placenta Praevia?
Answer: It can provoke catastrophic, life-threatening haemorrhage.

Q6. "Concealed" Danger

What are the signs of a concealed abruption if there is no visible bleeding?
Answer: Maternal tachycardia, hypotension, severe abdominal pain, and fetal bradycardia/distress.

Tasks 4 & 5

Grammar: Future in the Past ⏳

When discussing clinical cases, handover plans, or business strategies that changed, we talk about a plan made in the past. We use "was/were going to", "was/were about to", or "would".

  • Was going to: A plan that changed. "I was going to start a private clinic, but the overheads were too high."
  • Was about to: Something on the brink of happening. "We were about to cut when the heart rate recovered."
  • Would: A past prediction. "We knew the abruption would require a section."

Exercise: Fill in the gaps

Use: was/were going to, was/were about to, or would.

  1. I prescribe Aspirin, but she told me she was allergic.
    Answer: was going to
  2. The surgeon make the incision when the anaesthetist shouted to stop.
    Answer: was about to
  3. She thought private practice be less stressful, but she actually works more hours now.
    Answer: would
  4. I post that interesting case study on Instagram, but I realized it breached patient confidentiality.
    Answer: was going to
  5. They sign the lease for the new clinic when the landlord suddenly raised the price.
    Answer: were about to
  6. We explained that the praevia prevent a natural delivery.
    Answer: would
  7. I step down her care to the general ward, but her blood pressure spiked again.
    Answer: was going to

Q7. Red Flag Headaches & Reflexes

A pregnant woman presents with a severe frontal headache. You check her deep tendon reflexes and notice hyperreflexiahiperrefleksja (wygórowane odruchy). What does this indicate, and what specific questions do you ask to differentiate a migraine from pre-eclampsia?
Answer: Indicates CNS irritability (impending eclampsia). Ask about scotoma (mroczki) and epigastric pain.

Q8. Obstetric Cholestasis

A patient complains of intense itching on the palms of her hands and soles of her feet, especially at night. What blood tests do you order, and why is this condition dangerous for the fetus?
Answer: Order LFTs and Bile Acids. Risk of sudden fetal demise / stillbirth.

Q9. Gestational Diabetes (GDM)

Explain the term macrosomiamakrosomia (zbyt duża masa płodu) to a patient. Why does GDM cause the baby to grow exceptionally large?
Answer: Maternal glucose crosses the placenta; fetal insulin acts as a growth hormone.

Tasks 7 & 8

Grammar: Verb + Gerund / Infinitive 📝

Certain verbs demand a specific form after them. This is crucial for writing accurate medical plans and business emails.

  • + Gerund (verb+ing): recommend, avoid, suggest, stop (quit an action).
    "We suggest monitoring the heart rate." / "I suggest leveraging social media."
  • + Infinitive (to verb): decide, plan, agree, refuse, need.
    "The patient refused to stay." / "I plan to open a clinic."

Exercise: Complete the Sentences

  1. We strongly advise (avoid) heavy lifting during this trimester.
    Answer: avoiding
  2. She decided (launch) a medical blog to educate her patients.
    Answer: to launch
  3. I suggest (monitor) her bile acids twice a week.
    Answer: monitoring
  4. The consultant agreed (review) the ultrasound scan immediately.
    Answer: to review
  5. She stopped (take) her iron supplements because of constipation.
    Answer: taking
  6. I plan (transition) fully to private practice within two years.
    Answer: to transition
  7. He recommended (admit) the patient for overnight observation.
    Answer: admitting

Tasks 10 & 11

Grammar: Phrasal Prepositions 🧩

Both Medical English and Business English rely heavily on fixed prepositional phrases. Incorrect prepositions sound unnatural to native speakers.

Key Pairings: Associated with, Predispose to, Susceptible to, Present with, Secondary to, Protect from, Engage with.

Exercise: Select the Preposition

  1. Pre-eclampsia is strongly associated hypertension and proteinuria.

    Answer: with
  4. She presented to the emergency department a severe, frontal headache.
    Answer: with

    Answer: to
