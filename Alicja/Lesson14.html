Task 3

Reading: The Post-Fellowship Dip 📉

Read the text about the psychology of returning home.

Many doctors experience what is known as the "Post-Fellowship Dip" when returning to their home country. After months of high-adrenaline learning, adapting to a new culture, and gaining immense autonomy abroad, returning to your old hospital can feel restrictive. You may feel that you are ready to implement new protocols, but your senior colleagues back home might resist change. It is a pivotal moment in a doctor's career: you must gracefully transition into a senior role without alienating your team, ensuring that your new skills consolidate rather than stagnate.

Discussion: Are you worried about "reverse culture shock" when returning to your hospital in Poland? How will you handle a senior doctor who rejects a technique you learned in London?

Task 4

Grammar: Looking to the Future 🔭

When planning your career, advanced future tenses make you sound decisive and professional.

Tense Usage Example (Medical Career)
Future Perfect
(will have + V3)		 Looking back from a point in the future at an action that will be completed. "By 2028, I will have established my own private clinic."
Future Continuous
(will be + V-ing)		 Projecting yourself into the future to see an action in progress. "This time next year, I will be operating as a full Consultant."
