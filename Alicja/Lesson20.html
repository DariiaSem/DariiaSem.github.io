08. Grammatical Theory

Past Modals of Deduction 🕵️‍♀️

We use these structures when we are guessing or concluding what happened in the past. Highly useful for diagnosing returning travelers or reflecting on vacations.

Structure Meaning / Certainty Example (Medical & Travel)
Must have + V3 You feel almost 100% sure that something happened. "She has terrible erythema. She must have fallen asleep in the sun."
Might / Could / May have + V3 It's a possibility (Maybe yes, maybe no). "He has food poisoning. He might have eaten contaminated seafood in Thailand."
Can't have + V3 You feel almost 100% sure that something did NOT happen. "They can't have applied sunscreen properly. Look at those burns!"
09. Grammatical Application

Playing Detective 🔍

Rewrite the sentences using the correct Past Modal (must have, might have, can't have).

  • I'm sure the airline lost my luggage in transit.
    → The airline my luggage. must have lost
  • It's impossible that she drank tap water in Bali; she knew the rules!
    → She tap water in Bali. can't have drunk
  • Perhaps the patient forgot to take his malaria prophylaxis.
    → The patient to take his prophylaxis. might/could/may have forgotten
  • I am certain they overpaid for that hotel room.
    → They for that hotel room. must have overpaid
10. Role-Play Simulation

The Returning Traveler 🌴

Put your grammar and medical vocabulary into action.

Context: A patient (the teacher) has just returned from a 3-week backpacking trip in Southeast Asia. They present to your clinic with severe nausea, dehydration, and a strange, inflamed mole on their shoulder that changed shape while they were traveling.

You (Doctor) Start the consultation. Ask about their travel history and symptoms.
Patient (Teacher) "I feel terrible. I've been vomiting since I got off the plane. And honestly, I forgot to use sunscreen most days. This mole on my shoulder looks really dark now."
You (Doctor) Use a Past Modal (e.g., "You might have eaten..."). Assess the mole using dermatology terms (e.g., "erythema", "photodamage").
Patient (Teacher) "Are you saying it's melanoma? Should I be worried?"
You (Doctor) Use Hedging to calm them down, and propose a clear plan (dermatology referral / hydration).
