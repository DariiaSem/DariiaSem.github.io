The 5 Fatal Mistakes in STANAG Writing: (1) Using contractions: don't → do not. (2) Writing fewer than the minimum words. (3) Using informal vocabulary: lots of → a significant number of. 03 Writing Arsenal

Vocabulary: Formal Writing Language

Examiners reward vocabulary range. Replace everyday words with these formal equivalents. This is the single fastest way to raise your writing score.

I am writing toOPENER

The mandatory first line of a formal letter — states your purpose immediately.

"I am writing to confirm the details of the Ukrainian delegation's participation in..."

I would be grateful ifREQUEST

Formal polite request — softer and more professional than "please" alone.

"I would be grateful if you could arrange airport transfer for our delegation."

Furthermore / MoreoverLINKER

Adds a new, equally important point. Stronger than "also" or "and".

"Furthermore, I would like to request a quiet room, away from the conference hall."

With regard toTRANSITION

Introduces a new topic within a formal letter. Replaces "About" or "Regarding".

"With regard to transportation, we would prefer a minibus from the airport."

I would like to draw your attention toEMPHASIS

Highlights something important the reader must not overlook.

"I would like to draw your attention to our dietary requirements outlined below."

Yours sincerely / Yours faithfullyCLOSING

Sincerely — when you know the person's name. Faithfully — when you wrote "Dear Sir/Madam".

"Yours sincerely, Colonel O. Kovalenko"

proponents / criticsESSAY

People who support / oppose a view. Used in Level 3 essays to present two sides.

"Proponents of higher defence spending argue that... Critics, however, contend that..."

Taking all factors into considerationCONCLUSION

Strong formal conclusion starter — shows you have weighed evidence from both sides.

"Taking all factors into consideration, I believe that a balanced approach is most effective."

05 Model Text: Level 2 Letter

The Perfect STANAG Letter

Study this annotated model answer for the Level 2 Writing task. Every highlighted element is a marker's favourite — learn to replicate it automatically.

Headquarters — 3rd Mechanised Brigade, Ukrainian Armed Forces Warsaw, 14 May 2025

Dear Ms. Kowalski,

I am writing to confirm the details of the Ukrainian military delegation's participation in the International Military Cooperation Meeting in Warsaw, scheduled for 20–22 May 2025.

With regard to transportation, we would prefer to be collected from Warsaw Chopin Airport by a minibus or a NATO-arranged vehicle, as the delegation will be travelling with equipment cases. We are scheduled to arrive at 18:30 on 19 May. I would be grateful if you could confirm whether this can be arranged in advance.

Concerning our accommodation, we would require three single rooms and one double room. We have no specific dietary restrictions; however, one member of the delegation does not consume pork products. Furthermore, we would appreciate access to a meeting room on the morning of 20 May to allow the team to finalise our presentation materials.

With regard to the content of our presentation, the delegation will address the topic of Interoperability Standards in Joint NATO-Ukraine Operations. The presentation will include an overview of current Ukrainian defence doctrine, lessons learned from recent operational experience, and proposals for enhanced coordination with Alliance partners.

Please do not hesitate to contact me should you require any further information.

Yours sincerely,

Colonel O. Kovalenko

Head of International Cooperation, 3rd Mechanised Brigade

📌
What the examiner gives marks for: (1) Highlighted phrases = formal register markers (+vocabulary range). (2) Each bullet point from the task instructions is addressed in a separate paragraph. (3) No contractions anywhere. (4) The letter is approximately 220 words — safely above the 150-word minimum. (5) Salutation + closing = correct matching pair ("Dear Ms. Kowalski" + "Yours sincerely").
06 Grammar: Formal Structures

Grammar: Conditionals & Passive Voice

Two grammar structures are essential for formal STANAG writing: the Passive Voice (for reports and official statements) and Type 1/2 Conditionals (for proposals and requests).

Structure Formula When Used in STANAG Military Example
Passive Voice Subject + to be + Past Participle Reports, news, official notifications — focus on the action, not the actor "The ceasefire agreement was signed by both parties." / "All personnel are required to carry ID."
Conditional Type 1 If + Present Simple → will/can + Infinitive Real possibility — making requests, setting conditions in formal letters "If you require further information, please do not hesitate to contact me."
Conditional Type 2 If + Past Simple → would + Infinitive Polite proposals in letters — making hypothetical requests without demanding "It would be greatly appreciated if a meeting room could be reserved for the team."
Passive + Modal Modal verb + be + Past Participle Official requirements, regulations, and formal recommendations "All classified documents must be secured before personnel leave the premises."
✒️
Key Insight: Native English writers use passive voice to sound official and impersonal in formal contexts. Overusing "I" or "We" in formal reports is considered informal. Practise replacing "We did X" with "X was done" to instantly sound more professional.
07 Grammar Practice

Rewrite in Formal Style

Transform each sentence from informal/active into formal/passive style, or into a polite conditional. This is an essential STANAG writing skill.

  • Informal: "We sent the delegation list last week." → Formal: "The delegation list last week."
  • Informal: "Can you book a meeting room for us?" → Formal: "I if a meeting room for the delegation."
  • Informal: "We need a minibus from the airport." → Formal: "A minibus from the airport to the hotel."
  • Informal: "Please let me know if you need anything." → Formal: "Please do not hesitate to should you require any further information."
  • Informal: "We're going to talk about NATO cooperation." → Formal: "The delegation the topic of enhanced NATO-Ukraine operational cooperation."
  • Informal: "We got there on the 14th." → Formal: "The delegation on 14 May 2025."
✔
Answer Guide: 1. was sent  |  2. would be grateful if / could be reserved  |  3. will be required / is required  |  4. contact me  |  5. will address  |  6. arrived

09 Level 3 Essay Structure

The Level 3 Essay: Paragraph Architecture

The Level 3 essay is not about having the right opinion — it is about presenting a structured, well-reasoned argument in formal English. Think of it as a military briefing document.

Paragraph 1 — Introduction (~40 words)

Do NOT give your opinion yet

Introduce the issue. State that it is debated. Briefly mention both sides exist. End with a clear thesis statement of what you will argue.

"It is widely debated whether..." "In recent years, the question of... has attracted significant attention." "The issue of... is one that divides opinion sharply."

Paragraph 2 — First Argument / Side A (~65 words)

Strongest argument for one side

Present the first (often stronger) argument. Give a reason, then a real-world example or evidence. Do not use "I" here.

"On one hand, proponents argue that..." "A strong case can be made for... For instance,..." "Those in favour of... contend that... This is evidenced by..."

Paragraph 3 — Counter-Argument / Side B (~65 words)

Acknowledge the other side — but you can refute it

Present the opposing view fairly. You may end this paragraph by acknowledging a weakness in this side — setting up your conclusion.

"On the other hand, critics argue that..." "However, it should be noted that..." "Opponents of this view suggest that... Nevertheless,..."

Paragraph 4 — Conclusion (~40 words)

State YOUR opinion clearly here

Summarise briefly. State your personal opinion directly. End with a forward-looking statement or call for balance.

"Taking all factors into consideration, I believe that..." "In conclusion, while both views have merit, I am of the opinion that..." "Overall, I would argue that a balanced approach is most effective."
10 Model Level 3 Essay

Annotated Model Essay: Defence vs. Social Spending

This is a model answer for the real STANAG Level 3 prompt from your exam sample. Study the structure, vocabulary, and how both sides are presented before your own opinion is given.

Essay Prompt: "A government has a responsibility to ensure its citizens' safety. Some people think it should increase defence spending but reduce social benefits. To what extent do you agree?"

It is widely debated whether governments should prioritise national security through increased military expenditure at the expense of social welfare programmes. While both perspectives have merit, this essay will argue that a balanced approach is essential for the long-term stability of any nation.

On one hand, proponents of greater defence investment argue that without physical security, social programmes cannot function effectively. Countries facing active military threats — as Ukraine's experience demonstrates — must allocate significant resources to defence in order to maintain sovereignty. Furthermore, a strong military can deter aggression, ultimately preventing the far greater social costs of war and displacement.

On the other hand, critics contend that excessive defence spending diverts funds from healthcare, education, and poverty reduction — the very foundations of a stable society. A population suffering from poor healthcare and lack of opportunity may, in time, become more susceptible to radicalisation or social unrest. It should also be noted that military strength alone cannot address internal threats such as economic inequality.

Taking all factors into consideration, I believe that governments must strike a careful balance between security and welfare. In times of direct threat, defence must take priority; however, sustained social investment is equally vital to building the resilient, cohesive societies that make a country genuinely worth defending.

Word count: ~268 words ✓

Write Your Own Level 3 Essay

Essay Option A

"In some countries, a few people earn extremely high salaries. Some think this is good for a country, while others believe the government should control salaries and limit what people can earn. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay Option B

Choose this OR A

"Globalisation has brought about more positive economic impacts than negative ones. Even though it affects the world's economies in a very positive way, its negative side should not be forgotten. What is your view?"

Begin your essay here. Paragraph 1: Introduction (no opinion yet). Paragraph 2: First argument — "On one hand, proponents argue that...". Paragraph 3: Counter-argument — "On the other hand, critics contend that...". Paragraph 4: Conclusion with YOUR opinion — "Taking all factors into consideration, I believe..."

🎯 Target:250–300 words ⏱ Time: 40 minutes
⚠️
Common Level 3 Essay Errors — Avoid These: (1) Writing a one-sided essay with no counter-argument. (2) Using "I" in body paragraphs — save it for the conclusion only. (3) Starting sentences with "Because..." — use "Because of this..." or restructure. (4) Using "big" — use "significant / substantial / considerable." (5) Writing "In my personal opinion" — "In my opinion" is sufficient.
