Task 02 — Know the Format

The Listening Test — Anatomy

Before listening to anything, understand exactly what the enemy looks like. The STANAG Listening test has a very specific structure — knowing it removes 30% of your stress.

Feature Level 2 Level 3
Number of items ~30 short conversations/dialogues ~30 items — mix of short + longer monologues (reports, news, briefings)
Audio length Each clip: 15–40 seconds Short clips + longer passages (2–4 min)
Played Each recording played TWICE Each recording played TWICE
Answer format Multiple choice: A / B / C / D Multiple choice: A / B / C / D
Topics Daily life, schedules, workplaces, travel, simple instructions Military operations, international news, social issues, official reports
What is tested Main idea, specific facts, who/what/when/where Main idea, inference, speaker attitude, cause/effect, specific details in longer texts
Accents Standard British or American English May include other native accents (Australian, Irish, South African)
⚡
Critical Rule: In the real exam, you will hear each recording TWICE. On the FIRST listen: identify the topic and eliminate wrong answers. On the SECOND listen: confirm your answer and check details. Never leave a blank — if unsure, always guess. No penalty for wrong answers.

Task 06 — STANAG Listening Tactics

The 4 Listening Tactics

Listening exams are won before the audio even starts. These four tactics are the difference between guessing and knowing.

Pre-Read the Question

During the pause before each recording, read the question AND all four options. You are hunting specific information — not listening to everything equally.

Listen for Contrast Words

The correct answer often comes AFTER words like "but," "however," "actually," "in fact," "although." These signal a correction to what came before.

Ignore Distractors

STANAG always mentions 1–2 wrong options IN the audio. If you hear a word from an option, it doesn't mean that option is correct. The trap is designed to fool you.

Use the Second Play

First play: decide between 2 options. Second play: confirm. If still unsure, choose the more specific answer. Vague options are rarely correct at Level 3.

TRAP_TYPES.log
// The 3 STANAG Listening Traps — memorize these patterns:

TRAP_1: "True but wrong"
// The option is factually mentioned in the audio, but it does NOT answer the question asked.

TRAP_2: "Sounds like the answer"
// Uses the same words as the audio but changes the meaning slightly.
// Audio: "no mechanical defect found" → Wrong option: "the ride had no problems in the past"

TRAP_3: "Too extreme"
// Uses absolute words: always, never, completely, all, none.
// If the audio says "mostly" or "usually," then "always" is WRONG.
Question Phrase What You're Listening For Key Words to Catch
"According to the report..."Specific stated fact — not inferenceNumbers, names, actions, locations in the audio
"What is the speaker's opinion?"Attitude or emotion — how they feel, not just what they sayThink, believe, feel, sure, worried, confident, unfortunately
"Why did X happen?"Cause — what triggered the eventbecause, due to, as a result, following, caused by, led to
"What is true about...?"You must eliminate 3 incorrect optionsCheck each option against the exact words in the audio
"What will happen next?"Future plans mentioned in the audiowill, is going to, plans to, intends to, expects to

Task 09 — Grammar Module

Grammar: Reported Speech

Reported speech is essential for both the Writing test (letters, reports) and the Listening test (understanding what was "said," "announced," "confirmed," or "claimed").

Direct Speech Reported Speech (Tense Shift) Reporting Verb
"We are deploying troops."He said they were deploying troops.said
"NATO will send reinforcements."She said NATO would send reinforcements.said
"The ceasefire has been signed."Officials announced the ceasefire had been signed.announced
"We cannot confirm the details."The spokesman said they could not confirm the details.stated
"Our group carried out the attack."The militia claimed their group had carried out the attack.claimed
"The investigation is ongoing."Officials confirmed the investigation was ongoing.confirmed
⚡
Why this matters for Listening: In STANAG audio, you frequently hear phrases like "officials said," "the report confirmed," "the general announced." You must understand what was claimed vs. what is confirmed fact. The answer often depends on which reporting verb was used.
Task 10 — Grammar Practice

Convert to Reported Speech

Transform these military direct quotes into reported speech. Use the correct tense shift and an appropriate reporting verb from the box: said / announced / confirmed / claimed / stated / warned.

  • "The patrol will return to base by 0200." — The commander that the patrol return to base by 0200.
  • "We have detected movement near the eastern perimeter." — Intelligence officers that they movement near the eastern perimeter.
  • "Our forces did not fire first." — The general that their forces not fired first.
  • "Damage assessment is still ongoing." — The spokesman that the damage assessment still ongoing.
  • "All personnel must carry their ID cards at all times." — The officer that all personnel carry their ID cards at all times.
  • "The ceasefire was violated by both parties." — UN observers that the ceasefire violated by both parties.
✔
Answer Guide: 1. said / would  |  2. confirmed / had detected  |  3. claimed / had  |  4. stated / was  |  5. stated/announced / had to / must  |  6. confirmed / had been

Task 12 — Pronunciation & Fast Speech

Pronunciation: Reductions in Fast Speech

STANAG audio uses natural, connected speech. Native speakers "swallow" sounds and words. If you don't know these reductions, you'll miss key information in the listening test.

Written Form How It Sounds (Fast Speech) Example in Audio Context
going to"gonna""They're gonna deploy reinforcements next week."
want to"wanna""Command doesn't wanna escalate the situation."
have to / has to"hafta / hasta""All personnel hafta carry ID at all times."
could have"coulda""They coulda handled the situation differently."
did you"didja""Didja see the assessment report?"
sort of / kind of"sorta / kinda""The barge is kinda like a floating hotel."
them"'em""They left without notifying 'em." (= them)
and"'n" or "en""Officers 'n enlisted personnel — 284 total."

Practice Drill: Read the following sentence aloud at full natural speed, using all reductions: "I'm gonna hafta tell 'em they coulda reported this yesterday, 'n now I kinda hafta deal with it myself." — Your teacher will correct your rhythm and connected speech.

Speaking Tip: Filler Strategy

Don't say "eehhh" or go silent. Use: "That's an interesting point..." / "Let me think about that..." / "From a military perspective..." — These are natural AND buy you 3-5 seconds to organize your answer.

What Examiners Score

1. Fluency (do you speak smoothly?) 2. Vocabulary range (do you use varied words?) 3. Grammar accuracy 4. Task completion (did you actually answer the question?) All four are equally weighted.
