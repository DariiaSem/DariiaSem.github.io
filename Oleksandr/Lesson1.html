📚 Task 3 — Military Vocabulary

Core Vocabulary: International Security

These 8 words appear constantly in STANAG reading and listening texts. Master them — they are your ammunition.

deployverb

To move troops or equipment into position, ready for action.

"NATO decided to deploy additional forces to the eastern flank."

sanctionnoun/verb

An official penalty or restriction imposed on a country as punishment for breaking rules.

"The UN imposed economic sanctions after the ceasefire was violated."

ceasefirenoun

An agreement between two sides at war to stop fighting for a period of time.

"The two sides agreed to a temporary ceasefire to allow civilians to evacuate."

personnelnoun

The people employed in an organization, especially the armed forces.

"All military personnel must carry their ID at all times."

withdrawalnoun

The act of removing troops or forces from a position or area.

"The US announced the withdrawal of 3,000 troops from the region."

mandatenoun/verb

An official order or authority to carry out a specific mission or policy.

"The UN mission's mandate was to protect civilian infrastructure."

reconnaissancenoun

A military survey of an enemy's territory or position to gather information.

"A reconnaissance drone was sent before the ground forces advanced."

detonationnoun

The act of causing an explosive to explode; also used in accident/incident reports.

"The accidental detonation caused minor injuries to two servicemen."

🎯 Task 6 — Reading Strategy

The 4-Step STANAG Reading Method

Most students fail because they read the wrong way. Here is the proven tactical approach used by top STANAG candidates.

1

Read the Question FIRST

Before reading the text, always read the question. This tells you exactly what information you are hunting for. Do not read blind.

2

Skim for Structure

Read the first and last sentence of each paragraph to understand the text structure. This takes 20 seconds and saves 2 minutes.

3

Scan for Keywords

Find a keyword from the question in the text. Read only the 2 sentences around it. The answer is almost always there.

4

Eliminate Wrong Answers

The STANAG uses 3 types of "traps": statements that are true but not the answer, answers that are too extreme, and answers that mix true and false details.

🚫
The #1 Trap in STANAG Reading: The "Distractor" answer looks correct because it uses words from the text — but it changes the meaning. Always check that the answer matches the MEANING of the text, not just the WORDS.
Question Type What It Tests Your Strategy
"According to the text..." Factual comprehension — what the text directly states. Find the exact information in the text. The answer must be supported directly.
"What is true about..." Tests whether you can identify a correctly stated fact vs. a distorted one. Eliminate 3 wrong options by checking each against the text carefully.
"The purpose of..." Inference — understanding why something was written or done. Look for the author's main message, not just individual facts.
"Why did X happen?" Cause and effect relationships within the text. Look for words like "because," "due to," "as a result," "following."

Advanced Level 3 Text

✏️ Task 10 — Grammar Practice

Fill the Gaps: Tenses in Military Reports

Complete each sentence using the correct form of the verb in brackets. Choose the right tense based on the context.

  • NATO forces (deploy) additional troops to Poland last March after the security alert.
  • The Ukrainian delegation (travel) to Brussels next week for the Annual Security Conference.
  • Since 2022, the alliance (strengthen) its eastern flank significantly.
  • I (arrive) at the hotel by 10:00 PM and I (need) a quiet room for an early meeting the following morning.
  • The ceasefire agreement (sign — passive) by both parties after three days of intense negotiations.
  • The report states that all military personnel (require — passive) to carry their identification at all times.
💡
Answer Key: 1. deployed   2. is travelling / will travel   3. has strengthened   4. will arrive / will need   5. was signed   6. are required
