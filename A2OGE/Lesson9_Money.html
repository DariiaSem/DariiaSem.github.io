Task 3 · Vocabulary

Financial Words

📊

1. Budget

A plan that shows how much money you have and how you will spend it.

We need to make a budget for our holiday so we don't spend too much.

🤝

2. Loan

Money that you borrow from a bank or person, which you must pay back.

She took out a student loan to pay for university.

📈

3. Invest

To put money into something (like a business or stocks) to make a profit later.

It is smart to invest your money when you are young.

🐷

4. Savings

The money you keep in a bank and do not spend.

I used all my savings to buy my first car.

📉

5. Debt

The amount of money that you owe to someone else. (The 'b' is silent!).

He has a lot of credit card debt.

🧾

6. Interest

The extra money you pay when you borrow a loan, or the extra money a bank pays you for your savings.

The bank charges 5% interest on this loan.

Task 5 · Reading

The 50/30/20 Rule

What is it? Personal finance can be confusing, but the 50/30/20 rule makes it simple. It is a budgeting method that was created to help beginners manage their money.

How it works:
50% Needs: Half of your money goes to things you must have (rent, food, medicine).
30% Wants: Thirty percent goes to fun things (cinema, eating at restaurants, shopping).
20% Savings & Debt: The last twenty percent is invested, put into savings, or used to pay off old loans.

By following this rule, an "emergency fund" can be built quickly. An emergency fund is cash saved only for surprises, like if your car breaks down or you lose your job.

Task 7 · Grammar Theory

Passive Voice (Past Simple)

Last lesson we learned the Present Passive (is/are + V3). To talk about history, completed actions, or old financial decisions, we use the Past Simple Passive.

⏳ The Formula (Past)
Object + was / were + Past Participle (V3)
🔄 Active vs. Passive (Past)
Active: The bank approved my loan yesterday.
Passive: My loan was approved (by the bank) yesterday.
⚖️ Compare Present & Past
Present: The budget is planned every month. (Routine)
Past: The budget was planned in January. (Finished action)
