🗂️ Task 5 · Reading

A Decade in Cinema

Read the article about how movies have changed. Pay attention to how the writer compares different films.

Have Movies Changed Forever?

The last ten years have brought massive changes to the film industry. A decade ago, going to the cinema was the only way to see a new release. Today, streaming platforms are much more popular than traditional cinemas.

We have also seen a change in what audiences want. Superhero movies became the most profitable genre in history, with films like Avengers: Endgame making billions. These movies are louder, faster, and visually more spectacular than films from the past. However, some critics argue that their plots are worse and less original than older, classic films.

On the other hand, independent ("indie") movies are having a golden age. Thanks to digital cameras, it is easier and cheaper to make a movie now than it was 20 years ago. This allows new directors to tell unique stories. While an indie film might not be as exciting as an action blockbuster, it often features the best acting you will see all year.

📐 Task 7 · Grammar Theory

Comparatives & Superlatives

When we review movies, we love to compare them! Let's look at the rules for adjectives.

Base
"It is a fast movie."
Comparative
"It is faster than the first one."
Superlative
"It is the fastest movie of the year."
Adjective Type Comparative (comparing 2 things) Superlative (the #1 in a group)
1 Syllable (short)
cheap, old, loud		 Add -er + than
cheaper than		 the + add -est
the cheapest
Ending in -y
scary, funny		 Remove -y, add -ier + than
scarier than		 the + remove -y, add -iest
the scariest
2+ Syllables (long)
popular, exciting		 more + adjective + than
more popular than		 the most + adjective
the most popular
Irregular
good / bad / far		 better than / worse than / further than the best / the worst / the furthest
💡 Equality: If two movies are exactly the same level, use as + adjective + as.
"The sequel is as good as the original."

🎙️ Task 11 · Pronunciation

The Schwa in Comparisons

In fast American speech, grammatical words like "than", "the", and the "-er" ending are not stressed. They become a weak "schwa" sound (/ə/). Listen to your teacher and repeat.

-er than

Written: better than

Spoken: bett-er th-n (/ˈbɛtər ðən/)

Practice: "It's better than the book."

the -est

Written: the best

Spoken: th-best (/ðə bɛst/)

Practice: "It's the best movie of the year."

as ... as

Written: as good as

Spoken: z-good-z (/əz ɡʊd əz/)

Practice: "It's not as good as I thought."

🎭 Task 12 · Role-Play

Friday Night Decision

Student A wants to watch a slow, independent drama. Student B wants to watch a loud, action blockbuster. Argue your points using comparatives!

Student A: Suggest an indie drama. "I think we should watch Moonlight Shadows. It's supposed to be a masterpiece."
↕
Student B: Disagree. Suggest a blockbuster. Use a comparative: "Drama is more boring than action! Let's watch Explosion 4."
↕
Student A: Defend your movie. Focus on the acting or plot. "But the cast is better! And the plot is more interesting."
↕
Student B: Counter-attack. Talk about the cinema experience. "Yes, but action movies are the best to watch on a big screen!"
↕
Student A: Offer a compromise. Can you think of a genre you both like? "What about a thriller? It's as exciting as action, but smarter."
