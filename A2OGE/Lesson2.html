The Winter Survival Guide

Read the short health article below. Pay attention to the advice it gives.

How to Bulletproof Your Body This Winter

Winter is beautiful, but it brings cold weather, dark evenings, and the flu season. If you want to stay healthy, you ought to make some changes to your daily routine.

First, focus on your diet. You should eat foods rich in Vitamin C and Zinc, like oranges, spinach, and nuts. These strengthen your immune system. Secondly, many people stop exercising when it gets cold. This is a mistake! You had better keep moving. Even a 20-minute indoor workout can increase blood flow and reduce winter fatigue.

Finally, sleep is your best defense. You shouldn't stay up late looking at your phone. If you feel the symptoms of a cold starting, you ought to rest immediately. Your body heals when you sleep. If your fever lasts for more than three days, however, you had better see a doctor for a proper prescription.

📐 Task 7 · Grammar Theory

Modals of Advice

When we want to tell someone what is a good idea (or a bad idea), we use Modals of Advice. Notice how they change in strength!

Should
Normal Advice
(Good Idea)
Ought to
Stronger / Moral
(The right thing to do)
Had better
Warning!
(Or there is a bad consequence)
Modal Structure Example
Should / Shouldn't Subject + should + verb (base) "You should drink more water."
Ought to / Ought not to Subject + ought to + verb (base) "You ought to rest today."
Had better / Had better not
(Usually contracted to 'd better)		 Subject + had better + verb (base) "You 'd better take your medicine (or you will get worse!)."
⚠️ Common Mistake: We never use "to" after should or had better.
❌ "You should to go." -> ✅ "You should go."
❌ "You had better to sleep." -> ✅ "You had better sleep."
🖊️ Task 8 · Grammar Practice

The Doctor is In

Read the patient's problem and choose the best modal to give advice. Write should, ought to, or had better.

  • Patient: "I have a tiny headache."
    Doctor: "You drink a glass of water and relax."
  • Patient: "I am having an allergic reaction and I can't breathe!"
    Doctor: "We call an ambulance right now!"
  • Patient: "I want to lose a little bit of weight before summer."
    Doctor: "You start doing a 30-minute workout."
  • Patient: "I missed my last three doctor appointments."
    Doctor: "You apologize to the receptionist; it's the right thing to do."

🎙️ Task 11 · Pronunciation

Fast Speech & Reductions

In fast, spoken American English, native speakers often "squish" words together. Listen to your teacher and repeat.

Ought to

"Oughta" (/ˈɔːtə/)

"You oughta see a doctor for that cough."

Should have (Past)

"Shoulda" (/ˈʃʊdə/)

"I shoulda worn my warm coat today."

Had better

"'d better" (/bɛtər/)

"You'd better take your medicine now."

🎭 Task 12 · Role-Play

The Emergency Clinic

Student A is a stubborn patient. Student B is a frustrated doctor. Follow the flow!

Patient: Complain about a weird symptom. "Doctor, I have terrible fatigue and my left ear is green."
↕
Doctor: Give general advice (Should). "Oh dear. You should wash it with hot water and soap."
↕
Patient: Refuse the advice with an excuse. "I can't! Water makes it hurt worse!"
↕
Doctor: Upgrade to a warning (Had better). "Well, you had better take these antibiotics, or your ear will fall off!"
↕
Patient: Ask about a home remedy. "What about a natural remedy? Ought I to put lemon juice on it?"
