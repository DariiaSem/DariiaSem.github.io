Task 3 · Vocabulary

Medical Words

🤧

1. Symptom

A sign in your body that you are sick (like coughing or a fever).

A high temperature is a symptom of the flu.

🩹

2. Cure

Medicine or treatment that makes a disease go away completely.

There is no cure for the common cold, only rest.

📝

3. Prescription

A piece of paper from a doctor that lets you buy special medicine.

The doctor gave me a prescription for antibiotics.

🛡️

4. Prevent

To stop something bad from happening before it starts.

Washing your hands helps prevent illness.

🍵

5. Remedy

A simple, often homemade, treatment to help you feel better.

Hot tea with honey is a good remedy for a sore throat.

🥱

6. Fatigue

A feeling of extreme tiredness (much stronger than just "tired").

After working for 14 hours, she felt terrible fatigue.

Task 5 · Reading

The Winter Survival Rules

🐟 Rule 1: Diet
In winter, there is less sunlight. You should eat foods with Vitamin D, like fish or eggs. It is a good idea to drink plenty of water too.

🛏️ Rule 2: Rest
If you feel a symptom of a cold, you ought to stay home. It is the right thing to do so you don't make other people sick at work.

⚠️ Rule 3: Danger Signs
Most colds go away in a few days. But, if you have a high fever for more than three days, you had better call a doctor immediately, or you might have a serious infection.

Task 7 · Grammar Theory

Rules: Giving Advice

💡 Level 1: SHOULD (Friendly Advice / Good Idea)
Subject + should + Verb (Base)
✅ "You should drink water."
❌ DO NOT SAY: "You should to drink water."
⚖️ Level 2: OUGHT TO (Strong Advice / Moral Duty)
Subject + ought to + Verb (Base)
✅ "You ought to stay home."
⚠️ Level 3: HAD BETTER (Warning! Bad result if you don't)
Subject + had better + Verb (Base)
✅ "You had better take this medicine." (Or you will go to the hospital!)
Short form: "You'd better take..."
🖊️
Task 8 · Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Rule

Choose: should, ought to, or had better.

  • 1. Your friend has a small headache.
    You drink a glass of water.
  • 2. The patient cannot breathe! Emergency!
    We call an ambulance right now, or he will die!
  • 3. Your friend is sick, but wants to visit their grandmother.
    You stay home. (It is your moral duty).
  • 4. You want to lose a little bit of weight.
    You start eating more vegetables.

Task 15 · Grand Finale

The 30-Second Doctor

Choose one. Speak for 30 seconds.

Speaking Formula:

1. State the problem: "You have..."
2. Give advice: "You should / had better..."
3. Give a reason: "...because..."
A patient wants to stop getting sick every winter. Give a 3-step plan.
A patient never sleeps and drinks 5 coffees a day. Warn them (had better).
A patient asks: What is the best natural remedy in your country? Explain it.
