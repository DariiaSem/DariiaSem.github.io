Task 3 · Vocabulary

Stadium Words

🥅

1. Pitch

The flat area of ground used for playing sports like soccer, rugby, or cricket.

The players walked onto the green pitch.

🏃‍♀️

2. Track

A circular path used for running or racing.

Usain Bolt ran around the track very fast.

🟨

3. Referee

The person who controls the game and makes sure players follow the rules.

The referee gave the player a yellow card.

🏆

4. Tournament

A competition with a series of games until there is one final winner.

The World Cup is the biggest soccer tournament.

🧢

5. Coach

The person who trains and teaches the athletes.

Our coach told us to practice passing the ball.

👀

6. Spectator

A person who watches an event, especially a sports match.

There were 50,000 spectators in the stadium.

Task 5 · Reading

The Weirdest Sports on Earth

1. Cheese Rolling (UK): Every year, a large wheel of cheese is rolled down a very steep hill. Competitors run, fall, and roll down the hill to catch it. The first person to cross the finish line wins the cheese!

2. Wife Carrying (Finland): In this sport, men run a 250-meter track with obstacles while carrying a woman (usually their wife) on their back. The prize? The woman's weight in beer.

3. Extreme Ironing (Global): People take ironing boards to dangerous places—like the top of a mountain, underwater, or while skydiving—and iron their clothes. It mixes extreme outdoor sports with a boring home chore.

Task 7 · Grammar Theory

"Used To" (Past Habits & States)

We use used to to talk about things that were true or happened regularly in the past, but are NOT true now.

✅ Positive Sentences
Subject + used to + Base Verb
"I used to play soccer every weekend." (But now I don't).
"She used to be a professional swimmer." (She isn't anymore).
❌ Negative Sentences

Notice that we drop the 'd' because we use 'did'!

Subject + didn't use to + Base Verb
"I didn't use to like watching sports." (But now I do).
❓ Questions
Did + Subject + use to + Base Verb ?
"Did you use to play on a team?"
