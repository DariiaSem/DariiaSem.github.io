Task 3 · Vocabulary

Crime Words

💰

1. Robbery

The crime of stealing money or property, usually using force or fear.

The bank robbery happened at midnight.

🦹

2. Thief

A person who steals things secretly. (Plural: Thieves).

The thief took her phone from her bag.

🚔

3. Arrest

When the police catch someone and take them to the police station.

The police will arrest the suspect tomorrow.

🏛️

4. Court

The place where a judge decides if someone broke the law.

The thief must go to court next week.

😓

5. Guilty

Responsible for committing a crime. (Opposite: Innocent).

The judge decided that the man was guilty.

🔍

6. Detective

A police officer whose job is to discover information about crimes.

The detective found fingerprints on the window.

✏️

Task 7 · Grammar Theory

Passive Voice (Present Simple)

We use the Passive Voice when the action is more important than who does it. It is very common in news and crime reports.

🔧 The Formula
Object + am / is / are + Past Participle (V3)
🔄 Active vs. Passive
Active: The police arrest criminals.
(Focus is on the police)
Passive: Criminals are arrested (by the police).
(Focus is on the criminals and the action)
📰 Common News Examples
✅ "The museum is locked at 8:00 PM."
✅ "The diamonds are hidden in a safe."
✅ "The suspect is interviewed by a detective."

Task 11 · Role-Play

The Interrogation

You are texting a detective. You witnessed a crime! Fill in the blanks.

Detective Miller
Hello. We believe you saw the robbery yesterday. Did you see [anybody / somebody] run out of the bank?
You
Yes! I saw a man in a black coat. He was carrying a bag. Is he [arrested / guilty] yet?
Detective Miller
Not yet. The man [is searched / is searching] by our officers right now. Did he drop [anything / nothing] on the street?
You
No, I didn't see him drop anything. I hope you catch the [thief / court] soon!
