The E-commerce Boom

Read this short article about how adult shopping habits have changed. Pay attention to how things are compared.

Over the last five years, adults over 30 have completely changed how they shop. For busy professionals, time is more important than anything else. Online shopping is much faster and more convenient than driving to a brick-and-mortar store. You don't have to wait in line, and you can browse thousands of products from your sofa.

However, physical stores still have advantages. It is easier to try on clothes, and you can take the item home immediately. But overall, e-commerce offers the widest variety of choices and often the cheapest prices. For impulsive buyers, though, it can be the most dangerous way to shop!

Task 6: Grammar Theory

Comparative Adjectives

We use comparatives to compare two things, people, or situations.

Adjective Type Rule Examples
Short (1 syllable) Add -er + than fast → faster than
cheap → cheaper than
Ending in -y Change 'y' to 'i' and add -er easy → easier than
heavy → heavier than
Long (2+ syllables) Use more + adjective + than convenient → more convenient
expensive → more expensive
Irregular Comparatives:
good → better | bad → worse | far → further / farther

Task 8: Pronunciation Theory

Vowel Reduction & The Schwa /ə/

Based on Peter Avery & Susan Ehrlich's Teaching American English Pronunciation, English is a stress-timed language. Unstressed syllables are often reduced to a lazy sound called the Schwa /ə/.

The "-er" Ending

When we add -er to an adjective, that final syllable is completely unstressed. The vowel reduces to the Schwa /ə/. We do not pronounce a strong "R" or "E".

  • 👉 Fast + er = FAS - tə
  • 👉 Cheap + er = CHEE - pə
  • 👉 Bett + er = BE - d/tə

Task 10: Grammar Theory

Superlative Adjectives

We use superlatives to say that something is at the absolute top or bottom of a group (comparing 3 or more things).

Adjective Type Rule Examples
Short (1 syllable) Use the + add -est fast → the fastest
cheap → the cheapest
Ending in -y Use the + change 'y' to 'i' + -est easy → the easiest
heavy → the heaviest
Long (2+ syllables) Use the most + adjective convenient → the most convenient
expensive → the most expensive
Irregular Superlatives:
good → the best | bad → the worst | far → the furthest / farthest
