Task 3 · Vocabulary

Digital Words

1. Device (Noun)

A piece of electronic equipment (like a phone, tablet, or laptop).

I bought a new smart device today.

2. Battery (Noun)

The part of the phone that holds electrical power.

My phone is dead; the battery is at 0%.

3. Touch Screen (Noun)

A display that you control by touching it with your fingers.

Don't drop the phone; the touch screen will break.

4. Application / App (Noun)

A software program you download on your phone (like Instagram or WhatsApp).

I downloaded a new fitness app.

5. Notification (Noun)

A message or alert on your screen telling you something happened.

I got a notification that someone liked my photo.

6. Scroll (Verb)

To move text or pictures up and down on a screen to see more.

I spend an hour trying to scroll through TikTok.

Task 5 · Reading

The Addiction Test

Are you addicted to your smartphone? Read these 3 signs:

1. The Phantom Vibration
Sometimes you feel your phone vibrating in your pocket, but when you check it, there is no notification. Your brain is waiting for a message.

2. The First and Last Thing
You check your device the second you wake up, and you scroll through apps until you fall asleep. The bright touch screen ruins your sleep quality.

3. Social Isolation
You are sitting at a restaurant with friends, but everyone is looking at their screens. You care more about the digital world than the real people in front of you.

Task 7 · Grammar Theory

Rules: Too & Enough

Formula 1: TOO + Adjective (Negative Meaning)

Meaning: More than you want or need. It is a bad thing.

too + Adjective
✅ "This phone is too expensive." (I cannot buy it.)
Formula 2: Adjective + ENOUGH (Positive Meaning)

Meaning: The right amount. It is exactly what you need.

Adjective + enough
✅ "The battery is good enough." (It works fine.)
❌ DO NOT SAY: "The battery is enough good."
Formula 3: With NOUNS (Things)
too much / too many + Noun
✅ "I get too many notifications."
enough + Noun
✅ "I don't have enough memory on my phone."

Task 9 · Form Practice

Rewrite the Sentence

  • The phone is very expensive. I can't buy it. [TOO]
    The phone is to buy.
  • The battery is very small. It doesn't last all day. [NOT BIG ENOUGH]
    The battery is to last all day.
  • I have a lot of apps. My phone is slow. [TOO MANY]
    I have apps.

Task 11 · Pronunciation

Linking Sounds

In fast English, words connect. If a word ends in a consonant and the next starts with a vowel, they link. Listen and repeat.

Big + Enough
