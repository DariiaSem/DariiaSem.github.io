Task 1: Vocabulary & Speaking

Phrases for Hosting

A. Read situations a)-f). What expressions might we use in these situations? B. Match phrases 1-6 to situations a)-f).

  • 1. Make yourself at home.
    ↔
    You want someone to feel relaxed in your house.
  • 2. Excuse the mess.
    ↔
    You are apologising for how your home (or office) looks.
  • 3. Be my guest.
    ↔
    You give permission for someone to use something that is yours.
  • 4. Help yourself.
    ↔
    You are offering someone food.
  • 5. Have a seat.
    ↔
    You are inviting someone to sit down, maybe at work.
  • 6. Put your feet up.
    ↔
    You know someone is tired and you want them to relax.
Task 2: Speaking

Discuss the questions

Talk with your partner or group about your experiences.

Being a Guest

1. When was the last time you were a guest? What was the situation?

Hosting

2. When was the last time you had a guest? Was he or she a good guest? Why/Why not?

Cultural Etiquette

3. What type of things do good and bad guests do in your country?

Preferences (Added Question)

4. Do you prefer hosting a party or attending one as a guest? Why?

Limits (Added Question)

5. What is the longest a guest should stay at your house before it becomes annoying? "Guests, like fish, begin to smell after three days" - do you agree?

Disasters (Added Question)

6. Have you ever had a completely disastrous hosting experience or been an awkward guest yourself?

Task 3: Reading & Speaking

Tricky Situations

Read the situations. Have you experienced any of these? Discuss what you would do/say.

🌍 Situation 1

A friend of a friend is visiting your town and staying with you for a week. You don't know her, and she is from a different culture. You want to give her a warm welcome, but you are not sure how to greet her, what food and drink to offer, or what activities to recommend while she is visiting. You phone your friend to ask for advice.

🏠 Situation 2

You are staying with a host family abroad. The family seemed nice at first but now there are some problems and they don't talk to you much. You want to study, but the family is very noisy. They smoke inside even though you asked for a non-smoking house. In the morning, there is never any hot water left for your shower. You decide to talk to the mother of the family.

🥩 Situation 3

You are a vegetarian. You were invited to a barbecue at your boss's house. When you get there, your boss gives you a large plate of meat to eat. You do not want to offend your boss and her family by not eating their food. A close friend/colleague is at the barbecue, and you decide to ask for his advice.

Task 5: Grammar Theory

Prepositions of Time & Place

Review the rules for using in, on, and at.

Preposition: IN

Category Time Place Example
IN months, years, seasons - Paris is wonderful in April. I went in 2005. We ski in winter.
parts of the day - My train leaves in the afternoon.
- towns, cities, countries, continents, regions There's a castle in Edinburgh. My brother is in Mexico. What's life like in the desert?
- inside an object, room, or building Passport is in the drawer. Tickets in the living room.
Helpful hints: in a minute/an hour, in front of, in the middle (of), in the future.
Watch out! With verbs of motion (come, go, move, run, walk) we usually use to instead of in/on/at. (Was it hot when you went to Japan?)

Preposition: ON

Category Time Place Example
ON days, dates - I got a new car on Saturday. My birthday is on 19th March.
- islands, pages, top of an object, surface We stayed on Mykonos. Phrases on page 97. Keys on the table.
Helpful hints: on the beach, on the left/right, on my birthday.
Watch out! We say in the morning, but on Monday morning. We don't use a preposition with tomorrow, yesterday, etc.

Preposition: AT

Category Time Place Example
AT clock times - There's a bus at ten past three.
holiday periods - What are you doing at Christmas?
- exact places, addresses, activities in buildings What's it like at the North Pole? Lives at 132 London Rd. She is at a party / at the cinema.
Helpful hints: at the moment, at night, at the top/bottom, at the door/window.
Watch out! We use in for larger areas that surround us (in the countryside). We use at for smaller places/points on a journey (at the train station).

Narrative Tenses: The Dinner Party

Last weekend, I (decide) to host a dinner party for some new colleagues. Before the guests (arrive), I (spend) three hours cooking. While I (prepare) the dessert, the oven suddenly (break). I panicked because I (already / tell) everyone I was making a famous soufflé!

When the doorbell (ring), I (still / try) to fix the oven. I opened the door and saw my boss. He looked exhausted because he (drive) around looking for my house for 40 minutes. I invited him in and said, "Make yourself at home!"
