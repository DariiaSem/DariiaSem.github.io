Task 4

Grammar: Conditionals 2 & 3 🧠

Talking about "Imaginary Situations" vs. "Past Regrets".

Type Concept Structure Example
2nd Conditional Hypothetical Present
(Imagining life is different now)		 If + Past Simple, ... would + Verb "If I were a millionaire, I would buy an island."
3rd Conditional Past Regret
(Changing the unchangeable past)		 If + Past Perfect, ... would have + PP "If I had studied harder, I would have passed."

Your Turn: Complete the Thoughts ✍️

2nd Conditional (Dreaming):

If I could change one thing about my job, I [finish the sentence].

2nd Conditional (Hypothetical):

If I had a million dollars tax-free, I [finish the sentence].

3rd Conditional (Reflection):

If I hadn't moved to Poland, I [finish the sentence].

3rd Conditional (Past Choice):

If I had studied a different subject at university, I [finish the sentence].

Task 5

Game: Would You Rather? 🤔

Use 2nd Conditional to answer. "If I had to choose, I would..."

1. Skills

Would you rather be a master of every musical instrument OR be fluent in every language?

2. Time

Would you rather always be 10 minutes late OR always be 20 minutes early?

3. Life

Would you rather live in a world without music OR a world without movies?

4. Knowledge

Would you rather know the history of every object you touched OR be able to talk to animals?

Task 6

Deep Dive: Travel & Culture 🌍

Let's use 3rd Conditional (Past) and general narrative tenses.

🏠 Culture Shock

Think about when you first moved abroad. If you had known then what you know now, what would you have done differently?

✈️ The Best Trip

Describe your favorite travel experience. Why was it special? (The food? The people? The landscape?)

🚫 The Disaster

Have you ever had a travel disaster? (Missed flight, lost luggage). If it hadn't happened, how would the trip have been different?

🎯 The Future

If you could live in any country for 1 year (remote work allowed), where would it be?

Tasks 7, 8, 9

Extra Practice Tasks 📚

7. Phrasal Verbs (General)

Fill in: give up, look forward to, catch up, turn into, find out.

  1. I really look forward to my holidays.
  2. I need to catch up with my old friends.
  3. I want to find out more about this topic.
  4. Don't give up learning, even when it's hard!
  5. This small project might turn into something big.

8. Meanings in Context

What do the bold words mean?

  • "It was a blessing in disguise." (Something bad that turned out good).
  • "I'm feeling a bit under the weather." (Sick/Unwell).
  • "Let's call it a day." (Stop working for today).

9. Collocations

1. Make...a decision / a mistake.
2. Do...your best / a favor.
3. Take...a risk / a break.
4. Have...a good time / a chat.
5. Keep...a promise / a secret.
