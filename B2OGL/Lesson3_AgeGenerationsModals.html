Task 3: Advanced Vocabulary

Maturity & Aging

Click to expand: Open each box below to reveal the definitions of these advanced phrases about age.

To be in your prime (idiom)
To be in the best and most successful period of your life (physically, mentally, or professionally).
To come of age (idiom)
To reach the age when you are legally an adult and take on adult responsibilities.
Act your age (phrase)
A command telling someone to behave in a more mature, adult way, rather than being childish.
Age discrimination (noun)
Treating people unfairly simply because they are too old (or sometimes too young), especially in the workplace.
Maturity vs. Immaturity (nouns)
Maturity: Wisdom and sensible behavior that comes with age.
Immaturity: Childish, irresponsible behavior.
Elderly (adjective)
A polite and respectful word to describe people who are old.

Task 6: Grammar Theory

Advanced Modals of Obligation & Permission

As adults, our lives are dictated by rules, laws, and permissions. Let's look at advanced ways to express them beyond just "must" and "can."

Structure Meaning / Usage Example
be supposed to + V An expectation or rule that people often ignore. (Weak obligation). We are supposed to arrive at 9:00, but I usually get there at 9:15.
feel obliged to + V A strong internal feeling that you *have* to do something (moral or social pressure). I feel obliged to buy them a wedding gift because they bought me one.
make someone + V
(No "to"!)		 To force someone to do something. My boss makes me work late on Fridays.
let someone + V
(No "to"!)		 To give permission to someone. They don't let us wear jeans in the office.
be allowed to + V To have permission (focuses on the rule, not the person giving permission). We are not allowed to smoke inside.
managed to + V Succeeded in doing something difficult in the past. I finally managed to pass my driving test!

Task 8: Grammar Theory

Advanced Prepositions (B2-C1)

When discussing complex social topics like age or career, we use high-level adjective+preposition combinations. Memorize these pairs.

Prejudiced against

Having an unfair dislike of someone/something.

"Many employers are prejudiced against older workers."

Exempt from

Freed from an obligation or rule that applies to others.

"People over 65 are exempt from paying this tax."

Capable of

Having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do something.

"He is perfectly capable of managing the team."

Resigned to

Having accepted something unpleasant that you cannot change.

"She is resigned to working late every Friday."

Sympathetic towards

Feeling or showing pity and sorrow for someone else's trouble.

"We should be sympathetic towards people struggling with burnout."

Associated with

Connected with something else.

"Wisdom is usually associated with older age."
