Task 10: Idioms

English Sayings

What do these common English sayings mean? Do you agree with them? Do you have similar sayings in your language?

1. Blood is thicker than water.

(Family relationships are always more important and stronger than friendships or other relationships.)

2. A friend in need is a friend indeed.

(A person who helps at a difficult time is a truly reliable person.)

3. Like father, like son.

(Fathers and sons resemble each other, and sons tend to do what their fathers did before them.)

4. No man is an island.

(Human beings do not thrive when isolated from others; we need community.)

Task 11: Grammar Theory

The Four Present Tenses

Review the structure and primary usage of the present tenses in English.

Tense Structure Main Use Example
Present Simple Subject + V1 (-s/-es) Permanent facts, habits, or routines. He lives with his parents.
Present Continuous Subject + is/am/are + V-ing Temporary situations or actions happening right now. He is staying with a friend this week.
Present Perfect Simple Subject + have/has + V3 Past actions with a present result, or life experiences. He has met his neighbors twice.
Present Perfect Continuous Subject + have/has been + V-ing Actions that started in the past and are still continuing now (emphasis on duration). He has been talking to them for an hour.
Task 12: Grammar Strategy

Perfect Simple vs. Perfect Continuous

Use this logic to decide between the two present perfect tenses.

Did the action start in the past?
↓
Are we emphasizing HOW MUCH / HOW MANY or a finished result?
↓
Present Perfect Simple
(I have read 3 chapters.)
Are we emphasizing HOW LONG or the ongoing activity itself?
↓
Present Perfect Continuous
(I have been reading for hours.)
🚨 Important Exception: Remember Stative Verbs! (know, love, believe). Even if you want to emphasize duration ("how long"), you cannot use the continuous form with stative verbs.
✅ Correct: I have known him for 10 years.
❌ Incorrect: I have been knowing him for 10 years.
