The Ultimatum

You are negotiating a salary for a C-level executive position. You want $150,000. The company firmly offers $120,000 and says, "That is our absolute final offer. Take it or leave it."

03. COMPREHENSION

Fact vs. Fiction

1. The lead negotiator regrets not setting clear priorities at the beginning of the summit.
TRUE. "If only we had established our core priorities earlier," indicates regret about the past.
2. The opposition eventually agreed to lower the tariffs after the negotiator's concession.
FALSE. The text says "they refuse to yield," meaning they did not agree to compromise on the tariffs.
3. The advisor believes that walking away is a better option than signing a bad agreement.
TRUE. "I'd rather we walked away from the table entirely than sign a bad deal," her advisor whispered.

05. SYSTEM RULES

Wishes: Present & Future

1. Wish / If Only + Past Simple

Used to express a desire for a present situation to be different. It is contrary to current facts.

I wish / If only + subject + Past Simple (or Past Continuous)
"I wish we had more leverage."(Reality: We don't have leverage right now).
"If only the CEO weren't speaking so loudly."(Reality: He is speaking loudly right now).

2. Wish / If Only + Would + V1

Used to express annoyance or dissatisfaction with someone else's current behavior, wanting them to change it in the future. (Note: We cannot use 'would' for ourselves: "I wish I would..." is incorrect).

I wish / If only + subject + would + V1
"I wish they would offer a better price."(They are currently offering a bad price, and it annoys me).
"If only he would stop interrupting me."(He interrupts me constantly, and I want it to stop).
06. SYSTEM RULES

Wishes: The Past (Regrets)

Wish / If Only + Past Perfect

Used to express regret about a situation that happened (or didn't happen) in the past. It is impossible to change.

I wish / If only + subject + had + V3
"I wish we had walked away from the deal yesterday."(Reality: We didn't walk away, and now I regret it).
"If only I hadn't accepted their first offer."(Reality: I accepted it too quickly).
07. SYSTEM RULES

Advanced Alternates

At C1 level, we use specific phrases that force the Past Simple tense to talk about present preferences or urgency.

Would Rather (Subject 2)

When you state a preference about someone else's actions, use the Past Simple.

would rather + subject 2 + Past Simple
"I'd rather you didn't mention the budget yet."

(Compare to same subject: "I'd rather not mention it.")

It's (high) time

Used to say something is urgent and should have been done already. Always uses Past Simple.

It's high time + subject + Past Simple
"It's high time we signed the contract."

(Meaning: We need to sign it right now).

11. PHONETICS

The Emotion of Regret

When using "If only" or "I wish", native speakers use heavy emotional intonation. The auxiliary verb is often stressed to show the depth of the regret.

"If only I HAD known..."

The word HAD is stressed heavily, followed by a pause. The pitch drops at the end of the sentence to signify disappointment.

"I wish they WOULD listen."

The word WOULD is stressed to show extreme annoyance. Practice this sentence with your partner, expressing deep frustration.
