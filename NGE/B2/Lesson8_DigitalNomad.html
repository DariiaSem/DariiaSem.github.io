Eight Terms. Loaded and Ready.

These B2/C1 words are essential for discussing modern work culture. Study each terminal entry — the word, its function, definition, and a usage example from the current discourse.

01_autonomy.txt
> define —
autonomy
noun / uncountable
The right or condition of self-governance; freedom to make one's own decisions without being controlled by external authority.
"Remote workers consistently rank autonomy above salary in workplace satisfaction surveys."
02_precarious.txt
> define —
precarious
adjective
Not securely held or in position; dangerously unstable; dependent on chance or unknown conditions. Often used of jobs, financial situations, or legal status.
"The visa status of many digital nomads is often precarious — not quite legal, not quite illegal."
03_disruptive.txt
> define —
disruptive
adjective
Causing or tending to cause disruption; in business contexts, describing new technologies or models that radically alter established industries.
"Remote-first companies have proven to be as disruptive to commercial real estate as Uber was to taxis."
04_burnout.txt
> define —
burnout
noun / uncountable
A state of chronic physical and emotional exhaustion caused by prolonged stress, typically in a work context. Officially recognised as an occupational phenomenon by the WHO in 2019.
"Ironically, burnout rates among remote workers have been found to be higher than in office environments."
05_gig_economy.txt
> define —
gig economy
noun phrase
A labour market characterised by short-term contracts and freelance work rather than permanent employment. Workers are hired for specific projects or tasks.
"The gig economy has been celebrated as liberation and condemned as exploitation — often simultaneously."
06_attrition.txt
> define —
attrition
noun / uncountable
The process of reduction in workforce numbers through resignation, retirement, or death rather than through active dismissal. Also: gradual wearing down through sustained pressure.
"Some executives admitted they had hoped return-to-office mandates would quietly accelerate attrition."
07_proximity_bias.txt
> define —
proximity bias
noun phrase
The cognitive tendency of managers to give preferential treatment or higher performance ratings to employees who are physically present in the office, unconsciously disadvantaging remote workers.
"Proximity bias has been identified as one of the most significant structural threats to hybrid work equity."
08_leverage.txt
> define —
leverage
noun / verb
As noun: the power or ability to act or influence people, events, or decisions. As verb: to use something to maximum advantage. Borrowed from the concept of a physical lever amplifying force.
"Workers used the labour shortage to leverage better remote conditions from reluctant employers."

The Return-to-Office Wars

Read this adapted opinion piece from a business publication. As you read, underline or highlight every passive construction you find — you will need them for Task 7.

Future of Work Review — Adapted for B2/C1 Study
The Office Is Being Reclaimed — But at What Cost?
2024 | Remote Work · Corporate Policy · Employee Rights

For three years, remote work was celebrated as a revolution. Offices were abandoned, lease agreements were not renewed, and the future of work was being redesigned in real time. Companies that had resisted flexible arrangements for decades were suddenly forced to adapt. The pandemic had achieved in eighteen months what years of employee advocacy had failed to accomplish.

Then the pendulum swung back. In 2023 and 2024, return-to-office (RTO) mandates were issued by some of the world's most prominent employers — Amazon, JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, and Meta among them. Employees were told that in-person attendance was essential for collaboration, culture, and innovation. Productivity gains associated with remote work were questioned. The debate, it seemed, had not been resolved — it had merely been postponed.

Critics have been quick to note the paradox. It has been reported by multiple HR research firms that the primary motivation behind many RTO mandates was not productivity — it was attrition. Workers who refused the mandate were effectively being pushed out without the legal cost of redundancy payments. This tactic is now widely known and has become deeply resented.

Meanwhile, a parallel economy has been built by those who rejected the corporate model entirely. Digital nomads — knowledge workers who are no longer tied to a fixed geography — are now being courted by entire nations desperate for foreign income. Portugal's "D8 Visa," for instance, was specifically designed to attract high-earning remote workers. Croatia, Indonesia, and Georgia have followed. Similar legislation is currently being drafted in several other countries.

The larger question — whether the right to work remotely should be enshrined in law — has been raised in parliamentary debates across Europe. In Belgium, the right to disconnect was formally legislated in 2022. In France, employees cannot legally be contacted outside working hours. It is generally agreed among labour economists that the workforce has been permanently altered by the remote work experiment — the question is only by how much.

Task 7 — Grammar Theory

The Passive Voice: Full System Map

The passive voice shifts the focus from who does something to what happens to something. It's dominant in formal writing, news, and academic texts. Master all tenses, then master the advanced reporting structures.

Present Simple
facts / general truths
is / are + past participle
Remote work is considered a right by many employees. · Nomad visas are offered in 47 countries.
Present Continuous
happening right now / trends
is / are + being + past participle
New legislation is being drafted. · Workers are being pushed out through RTO mandates.
Past Simple
completed past event
was / were + past participle
The mandate was issued in January. · Employees were told to return by March.
Past Continuous
in progress in the past
was / were + being + past participle
The future of work was being redesigned in real time. · Offices were being emptied across the country.
Present Perfect
recent / relevant past
has / have + been + past participle
A new economy has been built. · The workforce has been permanently altered.
Past Perfect
before another past event
had + been + past participle
The debate had not been resolved — merely postponed. · The visas had already been approved before the law passed.
Future Simple
predictions / decisions
will + be + past participle
The new policy will be announced next quarter. · All contracts will be reviewed by HR.
Modal Passives
obligation / possibility / advice
modal + be + past participle
This issue must be addressed. · Employees should be consulted. · Benefits can be negotiated.

Advanced: Passive Reporting Structures

These C1 structures are used to report general beliefs, findings, or opinions without attributing them to a specific person. They are essential in journalism, academic writing, and formal speech.

Structure Pattern Example Used When
It + passive + that clause It is / has been + reported / believed / claimed / suggested / known + that... It has been reported that many RTO mandates were motivated by attrition. The source is unknown, collective, or deliberately omitted for neutrality.
Subject + passive + to-infinitive Subject + is / was + reported / believed / considered / expected / known + to + infinitive Remote work is believed to increase productivity. / The number is expected to double by 2030. More formal; the subject is placed at the start for emphasis or topic continuity.
Subject + passive + perfect infinitive Subject + is / was + reported / believed + to have + past participle The strategy is reported to have saved the company millions. / He is believed to have resigned voluntarily. The reported action happened before the reporting verb's time.
⚠️ Common Error: Don't confuse the two patterns.
✅ "It is believed that remote work improves wellbeing."
✅ "Remote work is believed to improve wellbeing."
❌ "It is believed remote work to improve wellbeing." — This mixes both patterns and is incorrect.

Task 12 — Pronunciation Focus

Stress Shift in Passive Constructions

Building on our weak forms work (Lesson 7), today we look at how sentence stress shifts in passive voice — and how native speakers reduce auxiliary verbs like is, are, was, were, been.

Active vs Passive Stress
The SUBJECT takes stress in active; the VERB takes stress in passive.
Active: "AMAZON issued the mandate." (Amazon = new, important info)
Passive: "The mandate was ISSUED by Amazon." (what happened = focus, Amazon = already known)
"been" reduction
/biːn/ → /bɪn/ in fast speech
"It has been reported" → "It's bin reported"
"Has it been approved?" → "Hazit bin approved?"
The /iː/ in "been" shortens to /ɪ/ — almost like "bin".
"was/were" in passives
Strong: /wɒz/ /wɜː/ — Weak: /wəz/ /wə/
"Employees were told" → "Employees wə told"
"It was designed" → "It wəz designed"
Auxiliaries in passive are almost always weak — the past participle carries all the stress.
Passive Reporting: Rhythm
Stress on REPORTING VERB + CONTENT
"It is KNOWN that remote work is GROWING."
"Remote work is EXPECTED to DOUBLE."
In passive reporting, stress the main content verb and the reporting verb — reduce everything else.
🗣️ Drill — Read aloud naturally:

"It's bin suggested that remote work wəz being used as a cover for deeper attrition. Workers wə expected to simply quit. Portugal is KNOWN to have offered more nomad visas than any other country."
Task 13 — Speaking: The Negotiation

The Return-to-Office Standoff

Act out this workplace negotiation. Try to use at least three passive constructions and one passive reporting structure naturally in your dialogue. Your teacher will count them.

HR MANAGER: Announce that a return-to-office mandate has been issued and will be implemented from next month. All employees are expected to attend four days per week.
↓
EMPLOYEE: Express frustration. Mention that productivity data has been consistently shown to support remote work. Ask why the decision was made without consulting staff.
↓
HR MANAGER: Acknowledge the concern. Explain it has been reported that collaboration has suffered. Suggest that a hybrid model may be considered if performance targets are met.
↓
EMPLOYEE: Push back. State that proximity bias has been well-documented and that remote workers are known to be systematically disadvantaged in promotions. Demand this be addressed before any agreement is reached.
↓
BOTH: Negotiate a final arrangement. Include: how many days will be required, how performance will be measured, and what protections will be put in place for remote staff.
