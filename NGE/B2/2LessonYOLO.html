Vocabulary

Advanced Adjectives & Nouns

Hover your mouse over the cards to reveal the blurred definitions.

Ephemeral

Lasting for a very short time; often used to describe fleeting experiences.

Adventurous

Willing to take risks and try new or exciting activities.

Hedonistic

Devoted to pursuing pleasure as a primary goal in life.

Repercussions

Unintended and often negative consequences of an action or decision.

Self-indulgent

Focusing excessively on one's own pleasure or desires without consideration for others.

Ambiguity

The quality of being open to more than one interpretation; uncertainty.

Superficial

Lacking depth or thoroughness, often used to describe relationships or experiences.

Mindfulness

The practice of being aware and present in the current moment, often for mental clarity.

Grammar Focus

Talking about Past Habits

When describing our past lifestyle (perhaps before we adopted a "YOLO" mindset), we use specific structures.

Structure Usage Rule Example
Used to + Infinitive Past habits, repeated actions, AND past states/situations that are no longer true. "I used to be a very superficial person." (State)
"I used to stay home every weekend." (Action)
Would + Infinitive Past repeated actions or behavior ONLY. Cannot be used for past states. "On Fridays, we would go out and pursue hedonistic pleasures."
Incorrect: "I would be a superficial person."
Past Simple Can be used for both past states and actions, especially if a specific time is mentioned. "In 2015, I traveled to Bali."

Grammar Focus

Conditionals: 2nd vs 3rd

Conditionals help us express hypothetical situations—perfect for discussing the repercussions of a YOLO lifestyle!

Type Structure Meaning Example
2nd Conditional If + Past Simple,
...would/could + Infinitive		 Unreal, hypothetical situations in the present or future. "If I had more money, I would travel the world." (I don't have money now)
3rd Conditional If + Past Perfect,
...would/could have + V3 (Past Participle)		 Unreal situations in the past (regrets or alternate history). "If he had said 'no', he wouldn't have experienced that." (He did say 'yes' in the past)

Task 5

Advanced Pronunciation: Word Stress & The Schwa /ə/

In advanced English vocabulary, placing the stress on the correct syllable is crucial. Unstressed syllables often reduce to a "lazy" vowel sound called the Schwa /ə/.

1. E-phem-er-al

Stress the 2nd syllable: i FEM ər əl

Notice how the 'a' and 'e' in the last two syllables become the lazy /ə/ sound.

2. He-do-nis-tic

Stress the 3rd syllable: hi: də NIS tɪk

3. Am-bi-gu-i-ty

Stress the 3rd syllable: æm bɪ GJU: ə ti

Practice Challenge

Read the following sentence aloud, focusing heavily on hitting the stressed syllables correctly and reducing the unstressed ones:

"The am-bi-GUI-ty of his he-do-NIS-tic lifestyle led to re-per-CUS-sions that were far from e-PHEM-er-al."
