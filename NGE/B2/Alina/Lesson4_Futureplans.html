Task 6: Grammar Theory 1

Will vs. Going To vs. Present Continuous

When making plans or predictions, we choose our grammar based on when the decision was made and how certain it is.

Going To Prior Intention / Plan
(Decided in the past)
Will Spontaneous Decision
(Decided NOW)
Present Continuous Fixed Arrangement
(Confirmed with others)
Form Usage Context Clinical Example
will + infinitive Spontaneous decisions (made at the moment of speaking) or general predictions without physical evidence. "The suction just broke!"
"Don't worry, I will fix it."
be going to + verb Intentions (planned before speaking) or predictions based on clear, present evidence. Look at that deep decay on the X-ray; it is going to require a root canal.
Present Continuous
(am/is/are + V-ing)		 Fixed Arrangements (usually confirmed in a diary, involving other people or bookings). Dr. Lee is attending the surgical conference in Berlin tomorrow.

Task 8: Grammar Theory 2

Future Continuous vs. Future Perfect

When discussing long-term career goals or technological advancements, we use advanced future forms.

NOW
Future Continuous
I will be working...
2028
Future Perfect
I will have finished.
DEADLINE (2030)
Tense Structure Usage Emphasis
Future Continuous will be + V-ing An action that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.
Future Perfect will have + V3 An action that will be completely finished before a specific future deadline (often used with "By...").

Task 10: Pronunciation

Reductions in Future Forms

Based on Teaching American English Pronunciation, fluent English speakers heavily reduce auxiliary verbs in fast speech. Let's practice the two most common future reductions.

Going to ➔ Gonna

The "ing" and the "to" merge into a weak Schwa /ə/.
Written: I am going to buy it.
Spoken: I'm gən-nə buy it. (gonna)

Note: Do NOT reduce it when "going" is the main verb of motion (e.g., "I am going to the clinic").

Will have ➔ Will've

The "h" is dropped entirely, blending into the L.
Written: We will have finished.
Spoken: We will-əv finished. (will've)

Listen & Repeat

1. What are you gonna do tomorrow?

2. By 5 PM, I will've finished the surgery.
