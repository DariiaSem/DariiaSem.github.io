Station 1: Passives & Relatives

Target: Advanced Passive Voice (Perfect/Modal) + Prepositions in Relative Clauses.
Combine the sentences or transform them as requested. Use the inputs to type your answer, then check the explanation.

  • L12: Advanced Passive
    Active: Someone must have leaked the patient's confidential data.
    Passive: The patient's confidential data .
    Answer: ...must have been leaked.

    Rule: Modal Perfect Passive = Modal + have + been + V3. This is used for past deductions where the doer is unknown.
  • L11: Passive Continuous
    Active: Tech companies are currently harvesting our attention.
    Passive: Our attention by tech companies.
    Answer: ...is currently being harvested...

    Rule: Present Continuous Passive = am/is/are + being + V3.
  • L14: Formal Relative Clauses
    Combine (Formal): This is the strict protocol. We base our cross-contamination rules on it.
    This is the strict protocol our cross-contamination rules.
    Answer: ...on which we base...

    Rule: In C1 formal writing, we do not end a sentence with a preposition. Move the preposition before "which" (things) or "whom" (people).
  • L13: Non-Defining & Whose
    Combine (Add commas if needed): Dr. Smith is giving a lecture. His research focuses on sleep apnea.
    Dr. Smith is giving a lecture.
    Answer: ..., whose research focuses on sleep apnea, ...

    Rule: "Whose" replaces possession. Because we already know his name (Dr. Smith), the clause is Non-Defining and must be separated by commas.

Station 2: The Gerund/Infinitive Trap

Target: Verbs that change meaning (L15) and advanced patterns (L16, L17).
Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb in brackets.

  • 1. The new algorithm makes influencers [post] highly deceptive content.
    Answer: post (bare infinitive)
    Rule: Make / Let + Object + Bare Infinitive (no 'to').
  • 2. We regret [inform] you that the biodegradable barriers are out of stock.
    Answer: to inform
    Rule: 'Regret to do' is used to formally announce bad news. ('Regret doing' means feeling sorry for a past action).
  • 3. She is not used [deal] with such severe patient backlash.
    Answer: to dealing
    Rule: 'Be used to' (meaning accustomed to) ends in a preposition ('to'), which must be followed by a gerund.
  • 4. During the long surgery, the dentist stopped [adjust] her posture.
    Answer: to adjust
    Rule: 'Stop to do' means you interrupt your main action in order to do something else.
03

Station 3: Articles & Quantifiers

Target: C1 Nuances of Quantifiers (L18, L19) and Advanced Articles (L17).
Circle/Type the correct option in the brackets.

  • 1. [ Every / All / Each ] of the assistants in the clinic must sign the new confidentiality agreement.
    Answer: Each (or 'All').
    Rule: You CANNOT say "Every of". You must say "Every one of", "Each of", or "All of".
  • 2. I couldn't finish the audit today because I had very [ few / a few / little ] time between patients.
    Answer: little
    Rule: 'Time' is uncountable, so we use little/much. Because the meaning is negative ("not enough"), we use "little" instead of "a little".
  • 3. [ A / The / Ø ] resentment is a common emotion when work-life boundaries are constantly crossed.
    Answer: Ø (Zero Article)
    Rule: 'Resentment' is an abstract noun being spoken about in a general sense, so it takes no article.
  • 4. [ Both / Neither / Either ] of the two composite brands are eco-friendly; we will have to look for a third option.
    Answer: Neither
    Rule: "Neither" is used to say "not one and not the other" when exactly two options are presented.

Station 4: Syntax Manipulation

Target: Cleft Sentences (L20) and Inversion (L19).
Rewrite the normal sentence using the dramatic syntax requested.

  • Inversion: Absolute Negative
    Normal: I have never seen such a cluttered and disorganized operatory.
    Never such a cluttered and disorganized operatory.
    Answer: Never have I seen...
    Rule: Negative Adverbial + Auxiliary (have) + Subject (I) + Main Verb (seen).
  • Cleft Sentence: Emphasizing the Reason
    Normal: He flinched because of the sudden pain.
    It was flinched.
    Answer: It was because of the sudden pain that he flinched.
    Rule: It was + [Emphasis] + that + [rest of sentence].
  • Inversion: Delayed (The C1 Trap)
    Normal: We realized the scanner was broken only after the patient sat down.
    Only after the patient sat down the scanner was broken.
    Answer: Only after the patient sat down did we realize...
    Rule: With "Only after" or "Not until", the inversion happens in the MAIN clause, not immediately after the time phrase.
  • Cleft Sentence: Emphasizing the Verb
    Normal: The assistant placated the nervous patient using mirroring.
    What the assistant did the nervous patient using mirroring.
    Answer: What the assistant did was (to) placate...
    Rule: What + subject + did + was + bare infinitive.
