02

The Viral Phenomenon

Read the article about "The Insta-Dentist." Click on the highlighted words to unlock their visual definitions. (Pay close attention to the verbs followed by -ing or to do!).

Ten years ago, a dental clinic's reputation relied on word-of-mouth. Today, many clinics encourage their staff to build personal brands online. The pressure of the algorithm makes modern dentists post highly produced content to gain clout in a saturated market.

Successful professionals leverage their expertise to educate the public. However, many new practitioners fall into the trap of trying to curate an impossibly perfect feed. They are used to displaying flawless veneers, but Gen Z audiences are increasingly demanding authentic content over polished marketing.

There are ethical risks, too. When a professional decides to endorse a commercial whitening product, they often face severe backlash from the medical community. Some purists strongly object to turning healthcare into entertainment. Yet, as the digital age evolves, it is crucial to maintain a digital presence.

04

The Algorithm (Grammar Rules)

You cracked the code in Task 3. Now let's formalize these advanced C1 gerund and infinitive patterns. These structures elevate your English from conversational to highly professional.

// PATTERN 01

Verb + Object + to do

Verbs like encourage, persuade, allow, advise, force, urge require an object before the infinitive.

"We encourage patients to leave a review."
// PATTERN 02

Make / Let + Object + do (Bare)

Make (force) and Let (allow) take an object and a bare infinitive (no 'to').

"The algorithm makes us post daily."
NOT: makes us to post
// PATTERN 03

Preposition + doing

Always use a gerund after a preposition (in, of, about, for, etc.). *Watch out for 'to' when it acts as a preposition!

"I object to turning healthcare into a joke."
"I look forward to seeing you."
// PATTERN 04

Be used to + doing

Meaning "to be accustomed to." The 'to' here is a preposition, so it requires a gerund or a noun.

"They are used to displaying perfect veneers."
