Task 2: High-Level Vocabulary

The Word Vault

Hover over these advanced C1 words to unlock their definitions. These words are crucial for discussing lifestyle, stress, and escapes.

Cathartic
Providing psychological relief through the open expression of strong emotions.
Vicarious
Experienced in the imagination through the feelings or actions of another person.
Sedentary
Tending to spend much time seated; somewhat inactive.
Ubiquitous
Present, appearing, or found everywhere at the same time.
Resilient
Able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult clinical conditions or stress.
Ephemeral
Lasting for a very short time; fleeting (e.g., the joy of a weekend).

Task 4: Grand Review Part 1

The Present Tense Console

Let's audit the 4 Present Tenses. Use this console to refresh your memory on the exact nuances.

Present Simple
Permanent states, habits, or universal facts.
"I work in a dental clinic in London."
Present Continuous
Temporary situations, actions happening right now, or annoying habits (with always).
"I am covering the night shift this week."
Present Perfect Simple
Completed life experiences (unspecified time) OR a past action with a finished result now.
"I have extracted 12 teeth today."
Present Perfect Cont.
Focuses on the duration of an ongoing action from the past until now.
"I have been standing for six hours."

Task 6: Grand Review Part 2

The Future Tense Console

Now, let's recalibrate the machine for the future. How do we plan the rest of our lives?

Will
Spontaneous decisions made NOW, or general unproven predictions.
"I'm exhausted. I will go home early."
Going To
Prior intentions (decided in the past) OR predictions based on clear visual evidence.
"I am going to ask for a raise tomorrow."
Present Continuous
Fixed arrangements (usually involving other people or tickets).
"I am flying to Spain on Friday."
Future Continuous
An action that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.
"This time next week, I will be skiing."
Future Perfect
An action completely finished before a specific future deadline.
"By 2030, I will have paid off the clinic."

Task 8: Grand Review Part 3

The Butterfly Effect (0, 1, 2, 3)

Before we mix them, let's review the standard conditionals.

0: The Fact
If + Present, Present. (Always true).
"If you don't sleep, you feel exhausted."
1st: The Real Future
If + Present, Will + Verb. (Highly possible).
"If I get the loan, I will buy the clinic."
2nd: The Imaginary
If + Past Simple, Would + Verb. (Unreal present/future).
"If I were rich, I would retire today."
3rd: The Regret
If + Past Perfect, Would have + V3. (Unreal past).
"If I had studied law, I wouldn't have been a dentist."
Task 9: Advanced Grammar

The Regret Eraser (Mixed)

At C1 level, we mix the 2nd and 3rd conditionals when a past action affects the present, or a general state affected the past.

Type A: Past Action ➔ Present Result

If + Past Perfect, ...would + Verb

"If I hadn't gone to dental school (past), I wouldn't be in debt right now (present)."

Fix it:

If I (not/stay) up late last night, I (not/be) so tired today.

Type B: Present State ➔ Past Result

If + Past Simple, ...would have + V3

"If I wasn't so resilient (general state), I would have quit the clinic years ago (past result)."

Fix it:

If I (speak) German, I (move) to Berlin in 2018.
